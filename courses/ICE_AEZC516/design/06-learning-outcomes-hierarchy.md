# Learning Outcomes Hierarchy: Advances in IC Engines (AE ZG516)

**Course Code:** AE ZG516
**Credits:** 4
**Institution:** BITS Pilani - M.Tech Automotive Engineering
**Version:** Final
**Date:** 2026-01-17

---

## COURSE OBJECTIVES (COs)

### CO-1: Engine Fundamentals and Performance Analysis
**Students will be able to:**
Analyze engine architecture, performance parameters, and calibration strategies to optimize torque, power, thermal efficiency, and fuel consumption for both SI and CI engines in real-world applications.

**Bloom Level:** Analyze, Apply
**Alignment:** Modules 1, 2
**Assessment:** Situated Learning Problems 1-2, Mid-term Exam

---

### CO-2: Air-Fuel System Design and Optimization
**Students will be able to:**
Design and evaluate air intake, fuel injection, and charge motion systems to maximize volumetric efficiency, mixture quality, and combustion performance while meeting power and emissions targets.

**Bloom Level:** Apply, Evaluate, Create
**Alignment:** Modules 3, 4
**Assessment:** Situated Learning Problem 3, Mid-term Exam

---

### CO-3: Combustion Process Analysis and Control
**Students will be able to:**
Analyze combustion processes in SI and CI engines, diagnose combustion-related issues using thermodynamic principles and diagnostic data, and apply advanced combustion strategies for efficiency and performance optimization.

**Bloom Level:** Analyze, Evaluate
**Alignment:** Modules 1, 4
**Assessment:** Situated Learning Problem 4, Mid-term and Comprehensive Exams

---

### CO-4: Emission Formation and After-Treatment Systems
**Students will be able to:**
Evaluate emission formation mechanisms and design integrated emission control strategies combining in-cylinder measures and after-treatment systems to achieve regulatory compliance.

**Bloom Level:** Evaluate, Create
**Alignment:** Modules 4, 5
**Assessment:** Situated Learning Problem 5, Comprehensive Exam

---

### CO-5: Advanced Technologies and Future Powertrain Systems
**Students will be able to:**
Assess the technical and economic feasibility of advanced engine technologies, alternate fuels, efficiency improvements, and hybrid powertrains for future mobility and industrial applications.

**Bloom Level:** Evaluate, Create
**Alignment:** Modules 2, 5, 6
**Assessment:** Situated Learning Problem 6, Comprehensive Exam

---

## MODULE OUTCOMES (MOs) AND SESSION OUTCOMES (SOs)

## MODULE 1: Engine Foundations - Build Your Engine

**Primary CO:** CO-1, CO-3
**Supporting CO:** CO-2

### Module Outcome MO-1-1: Engine Architecture and Power Fundamentals
**Students will be able to:**
Analyze engine architecture choices, calculate fundamental performance parameters (displacement, BMEP, thermal efficiency), and differentiate between torque and power to select appropriate engine configurations for specific applications.

**Maps to Sessions:** 1
**Maps to COs:** CO-1, CO-3

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-1-1-1:** Analyze engine architecture choices (configuration, cylinders, bore/stroke) and their impact on performance and packaging
- **SO-1-1-2:** Differentiate between torque and power and calculate BMEP and thermal efficiency from performance data
- **SO-1-1-3:** Apply thermodynamic cycle principles (Otto, Diesel) to explain compression ratio effects on efficiency
- **SO-1-1-4:** Identify major engine components and explain their functions in the power generation process

---

### Module Outcome MO-1-2: Breathing and Control Systems
**Students will be able to:**
Explain the 4-stroke cycle, calculate and evaluate volumetric efficiency, describe engine control system architecture, and diagnose basic control system failures using sensor and actuator analysis.

**Maps to Sessions:** 2
**Maps to COs:** CO-1, CO-2

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-1-2-1:** Explain the 4-stroke cycle in detail and analyze valve timing effects on engine breathing
- **SO-1-2-2:** Calculate volumetric efficiency and evaluate factors that affect air flow through the engine
- **SO-1-2-3:** Describe VVT strategies and their benefits across different operating conditions
- **SO-1-2-4:** Explain closed-loop control systems using sensors and actuators, and diagnose basic control failures

- **SO-3-7-3:** Analyze the interaction between fuel injection, charge motion, and piston bowl design for optimal mixing

### Module Outcome MO-4-1: SI Engine Combustion Analysis
**Students will be able to:**
Explain the SI combustion process including flame development and propagation, analyze engine knock including factors that promote it and mitigation strategies, and evaluate advanced SI combustion strategies.

**Maps to Sessions:** 8
**Maps to COs:** CO-3, CO-4

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):

---

### Module Outcome MO-4-2: CI Engine Combustion Analysis
**Students will be able to:**
Explain the four phases of diesel combustion, analyze fuel spray behavior and air-fuel mixing, evaluate the NOx-PM trade-off, and apply in-cylinder strategies to optimize emissions.

**Maps to Sessions:** 9
**Maps to COs:** CO-3, CO-4

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-4-9-1:** Explain the four phases of diesel combustion (ignition delay, premixed, mixing-controlled, late combustion)
- **SO-4-9-2:** Analyze fuel spray behavior including atomization, penetration, and air entrainment
- **SO-4-9-3:** Evaluate the NOx-PM trade-off and apply in-cylinder strategies (EGR, injection pressure, timing) to optimize emissions
- **SO-4-9-4:** Compare direct injection and indirect injection diesel systems
- **SO-4-9-5:** Assess the effects of fuel cetane rating, injection pressure, and multiple injections on diesel combustion

---

**Maps to Sessions:** 10
**Maps to COs:** CO-3, CO-5

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-4-10-1:** Compare ideal thermodynamic cycles (Otto, Diesel, Dual) and explain why real engines differ from ideal
- **SO-4-10-2:** Analyze Atkinson/Miller cycle principles and evaluate their implementation using variable valve timing
- **SO-4-10-3:** Evaluate advanced combustion modes (HCCI, RCCI, PCCI) including their benefits and challenges

## MODULE 5: Emissions - The Constraint That Defines Modern Engine Design

**Primary CO:** CO-4
**Supporting CO:** CO-3, CO-5

### Module Outcome MO-5-1: Emission Formation Mechanisms
**Students will be able to:**
Explain the formation mechanisms for NOx, CO, HC, and PM, analyze factors affecting each pollutant, and evaluate the NOx-PM trade-off in diesel engines to propose in-cylinder reduction strategies.

**Maps to Sessions:** 11
**Maps to COs:** CO-4

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-5-11-1:** Explain the formation mechanisms for NOx (thermal Zeldovich), CO (incomplete oxidation), HC (crevices, quench, incomplete combustion), and PM (soot formation/oxidation)
- **SO-5-11-2:** Analyze the factors affecting each pollutant (temperature, mixture ratio, mixing, oxygen availability)
- **SO-5-11-3:** Evaluate the NOx-PM trade-off in diesel engines and explain why in-cylinder optimization alone is insufficient
- **SO-5-11-4:** Identify emission sources for different engine operating conditions and propose in-cylinder reduction strategies

---

### Module Outcome MO-5-2: Exhaust Gas After-Treatment Systems
**Students will be able to:**
Explain the operation of Three-Way Catalysts and the importance of stoichiometric operation, design integrated diesel after-treatment systems (DOC, DPF, SCR), analyze DPF regeneration strategies, and evaluate SCR systems.

**Maps to Sessions:** 12
**Maps to COs:** CO-4

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-5-12-1:** Explain the operation of Three-Way Catalysts (TWC) and the importance of stoichiometric operation for simultaneous NOx, CO, and HC reduction
- **SO-5-12-2:** Design integrated after-treatment systems for diesel engines (DOC, DPF, SCR)
- **SO-5-12-3:** Analyze DPF regeneration strategies (passive, active) and evaluate soot/ash accumulation issues
- **SO-5-12-4:** Evaluate SCR systems including urea decomposition, ammonia storage, and NOx reduction efficiency
- **SO-5-12-5:** Compare after-treatment technologies (TWC, LNT, SCR) for different applications

---

Compare global emission regulations and explain RDE testing, explain OBD-II system architecture and monitored components, diagnose emission system faults using DTCs and sensor data, and evaluate OBD-II readiness monitors.

**Maps to Sessions:** 13
**Maps to COs:** CO-4, CO-5

#### Session Outcomes (SOs):
- **SO-5-13-1:** Compare global emission regulations (Euro 6/7, EPA Tier 3, BS-VI, China 6) and explain the evolution toward Real Driving Emissions (RDE)
- **SO-5-13-2:** Explain the purpose and architecture of OBD-II systems including monitored components and malfunction detection thresholds
- **SO-5-13-3:** Diagnose emission system faults using DTCs, freeze frame data, and live sensor data
- **SO-5-13-4:** Evaluate OBD-II readiness monitors and their role in emission compliance (I/M programs)
- **SO-5-13-5:** Apply diagnostic workflow to troubleshoot emission-related faults and avoid common misdiagnoses

## BLOOM'S TAXONOMY DISTRIBUTION

### Course Objectives Level:
- **Remember/Understand:** 0%
- **Apply:** 40% (CO-1, CO-2)
- **Analyze:** 60% (CO-1, CO-2, CO-3)
- **Evaluate:** 80% (CO-2, CO-3, CO-4, CO-5)
- **Create:** 60% (CO-2, CO-4, CO-5)

**Note:** Multiple COs span multiple Bloom levels (Apply + Analyze, Evaluate + Create)

### Session Outcomes Level:
- **Majority:** Apply, Analyze, Evaluate (appropriate for M.Tech level)
- **Minimal:** Remember/Understand (only where necessary as foundation)

## ASSESSMENT ALIGNMENT

### Formative Assessment:
- **45 MCQs** embedded in lectures (3 per session)
- Mix of Bloom levels within each session
- Immediate feedback for learning

### Summative Assessment:
- **6 Situated Learning Problems** (1 per module)
  - All require Apply/Analyze/Evaluate levels
  - Workplace-connected for authentic assessment

- **Mid-term Exam (30%):** Covers Modules 1-4 + Emissions basics (Sessions 1-13)
  - Tests: CO-1, CO-2, CO-3, CO-4 (partial)

- **Comprehensive Exam (40%):** All modules
  - Tests: All COs (CO-1 through CO-5)

- **Virtual Lab (15%) + Remote Lab (5%):** Experiential learning
  - AMESIM simulations (managed by different faculty)
