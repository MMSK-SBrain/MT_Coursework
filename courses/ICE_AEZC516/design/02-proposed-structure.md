# Proposed Course Structure: Advances in IC Engines (AE ZG516)

**Design Philosophy:**
- Start with what excites: Performance, Calibration, Real-world Problems
- Challenges anchor learning, fundamentals provide tools to solve them
- Each module connects to workplace reality
- Thermodynamics integrated contextually (not standalone)

---

## Module 1: Performance & Calibration Fundamentals (The Goal)
**Theme:** "What makes an engine perform well, and how do we optimize it?"

### Sessions:
1. **Engine Performance Metrics & Operating Characteristics**
   - Hook: "What separates a great engine from a mediocre one?"
   - Performance parameters, torque curves, BMEP, thermal efficiency
   - Effect of spark timing, compression ratio, load/speed on performance
   - MCQs: Calculate BMEP, interpret performance curves

2. **Engine Calibration Strategies**
   - Gasoline engine calibration (spark maps, fuel maps)
   - Diesel engine calibration (injection timing, EGR strategies)
   - Trade-offs: Performance vs Emissions vs Fuel Economy
   - MCQs: Calibration decisions for different operating conditions

3. **Engine Design & Operating Parameters**
   - Design parameters that enable performance (bore/stroke, CR, valve timing)
   - Thermodynamic cycles (Otto, Diesel, Atkinson, Miller, HCCI) - *contextual, not theory-heavy*
   - Link: How design choices affect calibration and performance
   - MCQs: Design parameter selection for performance targets

**Situated Learning Problem (Module 1):**
*"Optimize Performance in Your Fleet"*
- Identify a performance issue in your workplace engine (poor acceleration, low efficiency, etc.)
- Analyze performance data (torque curves, fuel consumption)
- Propose calibration or design modifications
- Predict outcomes and justify trade-offs

---

## Module 2: Emissions Challenge & Solutions (The Problem We Must Solve)
**Theme:** "The emissions challenge and how we fight it"

### Sessions:
4. **Emissions Formation Mechanisms**
   - Hook: "Why do engines pollute, and why is it so hard to fix?"
   - NOx formation (temperature-dependent chemistry)
   - CO, HC, PM sources and formation conditions
   - In-cylinder emission control measures
   - MCQs: Identify emission sources for different engine conditions

5. **Exhaust Gas After-Treatment Systems**
   - Three-way catalytic converters (SI engines)
   - Diesel oxidation catalyst, DPF, SCR systems
   - Particulate traps and regeneration strategies
   - MCQs: Select after-treatment for emission reduction targets

6. **Legislations, Regulations & OBD**
   - Emission norms evolution (BS-VI, Euro 6, EPA standards)
   - On-board diagnostics (OBD-II) requirements
   - Compliance testing and real-world emissions
   - MCQs: Regulatory compliance scenarios

**Situated Learning Problem (Module 2):**
*"Meet Emission Norms in Your Application"*
- Identify emission challenge at your workplace (failing norms, customer complaints, etc.)
- Diagnose root cause (combustion, after-treatment, calibration?)
- Propose mitigation strategy (hardware, calibration, maintenance)
- Estimate cost-benefit and regulatory compliance

---

## Module 3: Combustion Mastery (The Science Behind Power & Emissions)
**Theme:** "Understanding combustion to control performance and emissions"

### Sessions:
7. **Combustion in SI Engines**
   - Flame structure and propagation speed
   - Ignition fundamentals and spark timing
   - Knock phenomenon and fuel octane rating
   - Advanced concepts: Stratified charge, lean burn
   - MCQs: Combustion diagnostics, knock mitigation

8. **Combustion in CI Engines**
   - Direct injection vs Indirect injection systems
   - Fuel spray behavior and atomization
   - Ignition delay and premixed/diffusion combustion phases
   - In-cylinder strategies for NOx-PM trade-off
   - MCQs: CI combustion optimization scenarios

9. **Engine Diagnostics & Troubleshooting**
   - OBD fault codes and diagnostic protocols
   - Combustion diagnosis tools (pressure traces, heat release analysis)
   - Common failures and root cause analysis
   - MCQs: Diagnose engine faults from symptoms/data

**Situated Learning Problem (Module 3):**
*"Diagnose & Solve Combustion Issues"*
- Identify a combustion-related problem (misfire, poor combustion efficiency, knock)
- Use available diagnostic data (OBD codes, performance data, visual inspection)
- Root cause analysis: fuel quality, calibration, hardware?
- Recommend corrective action and validation plan

---

## Module 4: Air & Fuel Systems (The Enablers of Performance)
**Theme:** "Getting air and fuel into the cylinder efficiently and effectively"

### Sessions:
10. **Air Intake & Exhaust Systems**
    - Volumetric efficiency and flow through valves
    - Port flows and valve timing strategies
    - Turbocharging and supercharging principles
    - Boost control and wastegate strategies
    - MCQs: Turbo system selection, volumetric efficiency calculations

11. **Fuel Injection Systems & Mixture Formation**
    - SI Engine: MPFI vs GDI (gasoline direct injection)
    - Air flow measurement and fuel metering
    - Stoichiometric vs lean/rich mixture requirements
    - MCQs: Fuel system diagnostics and optimization

12. **Charge Motion & In-Cylinder Flows**
    - Intake jet flow and in-cylinder motion
    - Swirl, tumble, and squish generation
    - Effect on combustion and emissions
    - Crevice flows and blow-by
    - MCQs: Charge motion strategies for different engines

**Situated Learning Problem (Module 4):**
*"Improve Air/Fuel System Efficiency"*
- Identify intake/fuel system limitation (low volumetric efficiency, poor mixing, turbo lag)
- Analyze system performance (boost pressure, air-fuel ratio, flow data)
- Propose system upgrade or optimization (turbo selection, injector upgrade, port modification)
- Justify with performance/cost analysis

---

## Module 5: Advanced Topics & Future Directions (The Innovation Edge)
**Theme:** "Beyond conventional IC engines - future fuels and thermal management"

### Sessions:
13. **Alternate Fuels & Energy Transition**
    - Natural gas (CNG, LNG) - combustion characteristics and conversions
    - Alcohol fuels (methanol, ethanol) - properties and engine modifications
    - Hydrogen as fuel - challenges and opportunities
    - Future of IC engines in electrified powertrains
    - MCQs: Fuel selection for applications, conversion feasibility

14. **Engine Heat Transfer & Thermal Management**
    - Heat transfer in combustion chamber (intake, exhaust, cylinder walls)
    - Cooling system design and strategies
    - Effect on efficiency and emissions
    - MCQs: Thermal management optimization

15. **Engine Friction & Lubrication**
    - Frictional power losses (piston, bearings, valvetrain)
    - Lubrication strategies and oil properties
    - Link between lubrication and emissions (oil consumption, PM)
    - Low-friction design trends
    - MCQs: Friction reduction strategies

**Situated Learning Problem (Module 5):**
*"Evaluate Future Fuel/Technology for Your Fleet"*
- Select an alternate fuel or advanced technology (CNG conversion, hydrogen, Miller cycle, etc.)
- Analyze feasibility for your workplace application
- Technical assessment (performance, emissions, reliability)
- Business case (cost, infrastructure, regulatory incentives)
- Implementation roadmap

## Session-level Assessment Strategy

### Formative Assessment (Every Session):
- **2-3 MCQs embedded in lecture slides**
  - Placed after key concepts (not all at end)
  - Mix of: Recall, Application, Analysis levels
  - Immediate feedback to reinforce learning

### Summative Assessment (Module-level):
- **Situated Learning Problem** (one per module)
  - Students identify real workplace problem
  - Apply module concepts to solve it
  - Submit analysis + recommendation
  - Peer discussion in next session

### Mid-term Coverage:
- Modules 1-3 (Sessions 1-9)
- Focus: Performance, Emissions, Combustion

### Comprehensive Exam Coverage:
- All modules (Sessions 1-15)
- Open book: Emphasis on application and problem-solving

## Engagement & Cognitive Load Strategy

### Avoiding Boredom:
1. **Vary session types:** Theory → Application → Diagnostics → Future trends
2. **Real-world hooks:** Start each session with workplace scenario
3. **Immediate relevance:** "Why this matters to YOUR engine" in every session
4. **Peer learning:** Share situated learning problems across cohort

### Managing Cognitive Load:
- Each session: 3-5 key concepts maximum
- Thermodynamics integrated (not standalone lectures)
- Spaced repetition: Revisit emissions, performance across modules
- Worked examples before practice problems

### Work-Integrated Learning:
- Leverage students' workplace context as learning laboratory
- Encourage data collection from their engines
- Build portfolio of workplace solutions through situated learning problems
- Final project could synthesize multiple situated learning problems
