### Defined Learning Outcomes:
- **LO1:** Discuss in detail the basic functioning and component interaction in a modern internal combustion engine, specifically four-stroke cycles used in Automotive sector

- **LO3:** Describe the Engine operating conditions, Formation of mixtures, Combustion SI, CI Engines
- **LO4:** Identify the sources of Emission and mitigation strategies

## 4. Content Structure
### 15 Contact Sessions (from handout):
1. Emissions (Pollution formation, NOx, CO, HC, PM)
2. Exhaust gas after treatment (Catalytic converter, Particulate Traps)
3. Engine Design and Operating parameters
4. Air intake Exhaust (Volumetric Efficiency, Flow through valves, Turbocharging, Supercharging)
5. SI Engine Fuel metering and Mixture formation (MPFI, DI Engines)
6. Charge motion within the cylinder (Swirl, Tumble, Squish, Crevice flows)
7. Combustion in SI Engines (Flame structure, Knock, Fuel factors)
8. Combustion in CI Engines (DI, IDI systems, Fuel spray, Ignition delay)
9. Engine cycles (Conventional, Atkinson, Miller, HCCI)
10. Engine Operating characteristics (Spark timing, Compression ratio, Load/Speed)
11. Alternate fuels (CNG, LNG, Alcohol, Hydrogen)
12. Engine heat transfer and Friction/Lubrication
13. Legislations, OBD (Emission norms)
14. Engine Calibration (Gasoline and diesel engines)
15. Engine Diagnostics (OBD, fault identification)

### Sequencing Strategy:
- **HYBRID MODEL:** Challenges anchor the learning, group related fundamentals alongside
- **Start with what matters:** Performance, Calibration, Emissions (NOT thermodynamics)
- **Work backward from goal:** Performance optimization and emissions control as anchoring goal
- Students hate thermodynamics - integrate it contextually, not as standalone theory

### Theory/Practice Ratio:
- Theoretical lectures with work-integrated learning
- AMESIM virtual lab: Managed by different faculty (not included in session design)
- Virtual lab weightage: 15%
- Remote lab assignment: 5%

## 5. Assessment Structure (from handout)
### Evaluation Components:
- **EC1:** Theory Quiz/Assignment (10%) + Virtual Lab (15%) + Remote Lab (5%) = 30% total
- **EC2:** Mid-Semester Test (30%) - Closed Book - Topics 1-8
- **EC3:** Comprehensive Exam (40%) - Open Book - All topics

### User Requirements:
- **2-3 MCQs throughout each lecture** (formative assessment, embedded in slides)
- **ONE Situated Learning Example per module** - workplace problem students identify and solve
- NO standalone thermodynamics sessions (students hate it)
- Each session must connect to real-world application
