# Course Objectives & Learning Outcomes Hierarchy
## AE ZG516: Advances in IC Engines

---

## Course Objectives (COs)

### CO-1: Performance Optimization & Calibration
**Students will be able to:**
Analyze engine performance parameters and apply calibration strategies to optimize performance, fuel efficiency, and drivability in both SI and CI engines for real-world applications.

**Bloom Level:** Analyze, Apply
**Alignment:** Modules 1, 3, 4

---

### CO-2: Emissions Control & Regulatory Compliance
**Students will be able to:**
Evaluate emission formation mechanisms and design integrated strategies (in-cylinder, after-treatment, calibration) to meet current and future emission regulations.

**Bloom Level:** Evaluate, Create
**Alignment:** Modules 2, 3

---

### CO-3: Combustion Analysis & Diagnostics
**Students will be able to:**
Diagnose combustion-related issues in SI and CI engines using thermodynamic principles, performance data, and diagnostic tools, and recommend evidence-based solutions.

**Bloom Level:** Analyze, Evaluate
**Alignment:** Modules 3, 4

---

### CO-4: Air-Fuel System Design & Optimization
**Students will be able to:**
Design and optimize air intake, fuel injection, and charge motion systems to achieve target performance, emissions, and efficiency objectives.

**Bloom Level:** Apply, Create
**Alignment:** Modules 4, 1

---

### CO-5: Future Powertrain Technologies Evaluation
**Students will be able to:**
Assess the feasibility and implementation strategy for advanced engine technologies (alternate fuels, advanced cycles, thermal management) in specific industrial contexts.

**Bloom Level:** Evaluate, Create
**Alignment:** Module 5

---

## Alignment with Original Learning Outcomes (LOs)

The handout provided 5 Learning Outcomes. Here's how they map to the new COs:

| Original LO | Description | Maps to New CO |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| **LO1** | Discuss basic functioning and component interaction in modern IC engines, four-stroke cycles | **CO-1, CO-3** (Performance & Combustion) |
| **LO2** | Describe thermodynamic concepts governing IC engine operation and cycles | **CO-3, CO-1** (Integrated into Combustion & Performance) |
| **LO3** | Describe engine operating conditions, mixture formation, combustion in SI/CI engines | **CO-3, CO-4** (Combustion Analysis & Air-Fuel Systems) |
| **LO4** | Identify sources of emissions and mitigation strategies | **CO-2** (Emissions Control) |
| **LO5** | Discuss legislative requirements and on-board diagnostics | **CO-2, CO-3** (Regulatory Compliance & Diagnostics) |

**Note:** The original LOs are mostly at "Understand/Describe" level (Bloom's). The new COs elevate them to "Apply/Analyze/Evaluate/Create" levels, appropriate for M.Tech working professionals.

## CO → Module → MO Mapping

### Module 1: Performance & Calibration Fundamentals
**Primary CO:** CO-1
**Supporting COs:** CO-3, CO-4

**Module Outcomes (MOs):**
- **MO-1-1:** Analyze engine performance metrics and interpret performance maps for optimization decisions
- **MO-1-2:** Apply calibration strategies to meet performance targets while managing emissions and efficiency trade-offs
- **MO-1-3:** Evaluate the impact of engine design parameters and thermodynamic cycles on performance characteristics

---

### Module 2: Emissions Challenge & Solutions
**Primary CO:** CO-2
**Supporting COs:** CO-3

**Module Outcomes (MOs):**
- **MO-2-1:** Identify and analyze formation mechanisms for major pollutants (NOx, CO, HC, PM)
- **MO-2-2:** Design after-treatment system strategies to meet emission targets for SI and CI engines
- **MO-2-3:** Evaluate regulatory compliance and implement OBD systems for emission monitoring

---

### Module 3: Combustion Mastery
**Primary CO:** CO-3
**Supporting COs:** CO-1, CO-2

**Module Outcomes (MOs):**
- **MO-3-1:** Analyze combustion processes in SI engines, including flame propagation, knock, and fuel effects
- **MO-3-2:** Analyze combustion processes in CI engines, including injection, ignition delay, and mixing-controlled burn
- **MO-3-3:** Diagnose engine faults using combustion analysis, performance data, and OBD diagnostic tools

---

### Module 4: Air & Fuel Systems
**Primary CO:** CO-4
**Supporting COs:** CO-1, CO-3

**Module Outcomes (MOs):**
- **MO-4-1:** Design and optimize air intake and exhaust systems, including boosting strategies, to maximize volumetric efficiency
- **MO-4-2:** Evaluate fuel injection system technologies (MPFI, GDI, common rail) and apply them to meet performance and emission targets
- **MO-4-3:** Apply charge motion strategies (swirl, tumble, squish) to enhance combustion and reduce emissions

---

### Module 5: Advanced Topics & Future Directions
**Primary CO:** CO-5
**Supporting COs:** CO-1, CO-2

**Module Outcomes (MOs):**
- **MO-5-1:** Evaluate the technical and economic feasibility of alternate fuels (CNG, hydrogen, alcohols) for specific applications
- **MO-5-2:** Apply thermal management and friction reduction strategies to improve engine efficiency
- **MO-5-3:** Assess emerging IC engine technologies in the context of future mobility and emissions regulations

## Session Outcomes (SOs) - Summary

Each session will have 2-3 specific Session Outcomes (SOs) that contribute to the Module Outcomes. These will be detailed in the final template.

**Example format:**

- **SO-1-1-2:** Interpret torque and power curves to identify performance characteristics
- **SO-1-2-1:** Apply spark timing maps to optimize SI engine performance
- *...and so on for all 15 sessions*
