# REVISED Course Structure - Physics & Math Foundation First

## Pedagogical Rationale
For students with **no automotive or mechanical background**, start with familiar physics and mathematics to build confidence before introducing complex automotive systems.

---

## NEW MODULE STRUCTURE (20 Sessions)

### **MODULE 1: VEHICLE MOTION FUNDAMENTALS**
**Sessions 1-3** | Build confidence with physics & math
**Philosophy:** Start with "Why do we need power?" before "How do engines work?"

#### Session 1: Forces Acting on a Moving Vehicle
- Resistance forces (rolling, aerodynamic, gradient)
- Newton's laws applied to vehicles
- Calculate force requirements for different driving scenarios
- Introduction to vehicle performance metrics

#### Session 2: Tires & Friction Fundamentals
- Tire construction and types
- Friction circle concept
- Normal force, friction coefficient
- Longitudinal vs lateral tire forces
- Slip, grip, and traction limits

#### Session 3: From Forces to Power Requirements
- Force → Torque (wheel radius relationship)
- Torque → Power (rotational motion)
- Vehicle performance calculations (acceleration, top speed, gradeability)
- **Conclusion:** "Now we know HOW MUCH power/torque we need. Next: HOW to generate it?"

---

### **MODULE 2: POWERTRAIN SYSTEMS**
**Sessions 4-8** | Now they understand WHY power is needed

#### Session 4: Introduction to IC Engines (Simplified)
- Engine as a torque-generating device
- Basic components (piston, cylinder, crankshaft)
- 4-stroke cycle (simple mechanical view, no thermodynamics)
- Engine torque and power curves
- Engine capacity and displacement

#### Session 5: Engine Support Systems - Cooling & Lubrication
- **Why cooling?** Heat generation in engines
- Cooling systems (air-cooled, liquid-cooled)
- **Why lubrication?** Friction and wear
- Lubrication systems and oil properties
- Thermal management basics

#### Session 6: Engine Performance & Fuel Systems
- Engine performance maps (torque, power, BSFC)
- Fuel delivery systems (carburetor, fuel injection basics)
- Air intake systems
- Emission basics (simple overview)

#### Session 7: Transmission Systems - Part 1
- **Why transmission?** Matching engine output to wheel requirements
- Gear ratios and their effect on torque/speed
- Manual transmission basics
- Clutch operation
- Calculate transmission ratios

#### Session 8: Transmission Systems - Part 2 & Alternatives
- Automatic transmission basics
- CVT, DCT concepts
- Differential and final drive
- Brief introduction to electric powertrains
- Comparison: IC vs Electric characteristics

---

### **MODULE 3: CHASSIS DYNAMICS & CONTROL**
**Sessions 9-12** | Math-based approach to vehicle motion control

#### Session 9: Vehicle Dynamics Fundamentals
- Longitudinal dynamics (acceleration, braking)
- Load transfer during acceleration/braking
- Weight distribution and its effects
- Performance calculations (0-100 km/h, braking distance)

#### Session 10: Braking Systems
- Braking physics (friction, deceleration forces)
- Braking force requirements and distribution
- Hydraulic brake systems
- Disc vs drum brakes
- ABS fundamentals (wheel slip control)

#### Session 11: Steering Systems
- Steering geometry (Ackermann principle)
- Steering angles and turning radius
- Manual steering mechanisms
- Power steering (hydraulic, electric)
- Steering forces and feedback

#### Session 12: Suspension Systems
- Spring-mass-damper systems (vibration basics)
- Suspension types (independent, dependent)
- Springs and dampers
- Anti-roll bars
- Ride comfort vs handling trade-off

---

### **MODULE 4: VEHICLE ELECTRICAL & CONTROL SYSTEMS**
**Sessions 13-15** | Electronics and automation

#### Session 13: Electrical Architecture
- 12V/24V electrical systems
- Battery technology and specifications
- Alternator and charging system
- Starter motor
- Electrical loads and power distribution

#### Session 14: Vehicle Control Systems
- Introduction to ECU (Electronic Control Unit)
- Sensors (temperature, pressure, position, speed)
- Actuators (fuel injectors, throttle valve)
- Control loops and feedback
- Engine management basics

#### Session 15: Vehicle Communication Networks
- Need for automotive networks
- CAN bus fundamentals
- LIN bus basics
- OBD-II diagnostics
- Network architecture

---

### **MODULE 5: VEHICLE ARCHITECTURE & COMFORT**
**Sessions 16-18** | Complete vehicle systems

#### Session 16: Chassis & Body Architecture
- Chassis types (monocoque, ladder frame)
- Body construction and materials
- Weight distribution analysis
- Crash structures and crumple zones
- Vehicle classification

#### Session 17: HVAC Systems
- HVAC fundamentals
- Refrigeration cycle (thermodynamics application)
- HVAC components (compressor, evaporator, condenser)
- Climate control systems
- Cabin air quality

#### Session 18: Passive Safety Systems
- Crash safety principles
- Seatbelt systems
- Airbag systems and deployment
- Pedestrian protection
- Safety testing standards (NCAP)

---

### **MODULE 6: ADVANCED SAFETY & EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES**
**Sessions 19-20** | Future of mobility

#### Session 19: Active Safety & Vehicle Stability
- Electronic Stability Control (ESC)
- Traction Control System (TCS)
- Integration with ABS and braking
- Yaw moment control
- Cornering dynamics

#### Session 20: Introduction to ADAS & Future Mobility
- ADAS overview (ACC, LKA, AEB - brief only)
- Sensor technologies (camera, radar, ultrasonic)
- SAE autonomy levels (L0-L5)
- Electric vehicles (brief overview)
- Future mobility trends

## KEY CHANGES FROM ORIGINAL STRUCTURE

### 1. **Sessions 1-3: NEW Foundation Module**
- **Original:** Started with vehicle architecture and chassis types
- **Revised:** Start with basic physics (forces, friction, power calculations)
- **Benefit:** Students use familiar math/physics before complex systems

### 2. **Tires Introduced Early (Session 2)**
- **Rationale:** Fundamental to understanding all vehicle dynamics
- **Connects to:** Braking (Session 10), Steering (Session 11), Dynamics (Session 9)

### 3. **Engines Delayed to Session 4**
- **Original:** Deep thermodynamics in Session 4
- **Revised:** Simple mechanical view first, no Otto/Diesel cycles initially
- **Benefit:** Reduces cognitive load, focuses on function over theory

### 4. **Cooling/Lubrication Right After Engines (Session 5)**
- **Rationale:** Logical connection - support systems for what they just learned
- **As you requested:** Keeps related topics together

### 5. **Math-Based Chassis Systems (Sessions 9-12)**
- **Braking:** Friction calculations, deceleration forces
- **Steering:** Geometry, angles, turning radius
- **Suspension:** Spring-mass-damper (familiar from physics)
- **Benefit:** Builds on Session 1-3 foundation

### 6. **Logical Progression Throughout**
```
WHY (Sessions 1-3) → HOW (Sessions 4-8) → CONTROL (Sessions 9-15) → INTEGRATION (Sessions 16-20)
```

## BLOOM'S TAXONOMY TARGET

For students with no background, appropriate distribution:
- **Remember:** 10-15% (basic definitions, classifications)
- **Understand:** 60-65% (explanations, comparisons)
- **Apply:** 20-25% (calculations, numerical problems)
- **Analyze:** 5-10% (system analysis, trade-offs)
