## 3. Learning Outcomes

### **Course Objectives (CO):**

**CO1:** Study main components/systems of an automobile (engine, transmission, drive-axle system, suspension system, brake system, etc.)

**CO2:** Understand fundamental working principles of different systems

**CO3:** Learn performance analysis along with working and important aspects of various components of automotive vehicles

**CO4:** Get acquainted with advanced concepts through projects and assignments

### **What Learners Will DO (After Course Completion):**

**LO1:** Recognize and identify different vehicle systems and components

**LO2:** Analyze the functions and evaluate the performance of vehicle systems

**LO3:** Understand importance of each system and how it affects safety, reliability, and performance of vehicle

**LO4:** Apply technical knowledge and skills to analyze and design automotive systems

**LO5:** Perform engineering calculations for powertrain, dynamics, and thermal systems

### **Critical Skills:**

1. **Analytical Skills:**
   - Analyze vehicle system performance
   - Perform engineering calculations for force, torque, power
   - Evaluate trade-offs in system design

2. **Technical Understanding:**
   - Understand IC engine operation and control
   - Understand transmission systems and gear ratios
   - Understand vehicle dynamics and handling
   - Understand electrical and electronic control systems
   - Understand vehicle communication networks (CAN/LIN)

3. **Integration Skills:**
   - Understand how different subsystems interact
   - Analyze system-level vehicle behavior
   - Understand control architecture and sensor-actuator integration

4. **Problem-Solving:**
   - Diagnose system issues using engineering principles
   - Apply knowledge to real-world automotive problems
   - Perform numerical analysis and calculations

### **Nice-to-Have:**

- Hands-on experience with vehicle systems (limited - mostly theoretical course)
- Exposure to automotive industry standards and practices
- Understanding of emerging technologies (EV, ADAS - introductory level)

## 4. Content Structure

### **Main Modules:**

**Module 1: Foundation & Vehicle Architecture (Sessions 1-3)**
- Introduction to automotive systems
- Chassis and body systems
- Powertrain architecture and requirements

**Module 2: Powertrain & Engine Support Systems (Sessions 4-8)**
- IC Engine fundamentals (Part 1 & 2)
- Engine performance and emissions
- Cooling and lubrication systems
- Introduction to electric and hybrid vehicles

**Module 3: Power Transmission (Sessions 9-10)**
- Transmission systems Part 1 (Manual, clutch)
- Transmission systems Part 2 (Automatic, CVT, DCT, differential)

**Module 4: Vehicle Electrical & Control Systems (Sessions 11-13)**
- Vehicle electrical systems
- Vehicle control architecture (ECU, sensors, actuators)
- Vehicle communication networks (CAN, LIN, OBD-II)

**Module 5: Vehicle Dynamics & Chassis Systems (Sessions 14-17)**
- Vehicle dynamics fundamentals
- Suspension systems
- Steering systems
- Brake systems

**Module 6: Comfort & Safety Systems (Sessions 18-20)**
- HVAC systems
- Passive safety systems
- Introduction to active safety and ADAS

### **Sequencing Rationale:**

1. **Foundation First:** Overview and architecture to understand the big picture
2. **Power Generation:** Engine systems and support (cooling, lubrication)
3. **Power Transmission:** How power flows to wheels
4. **Control Layer:** Electrical → Electronics → Networks
5. **Dynamics Foundation:** Physics of vehicle motion
6. **Motion Control:** Chassis systems (suspension, steering, brakes)
7. **Comfort & Safety:** Higher-level systems that integrate everything

### **Logical Progression:**
- Start with mechanical systems (simpler, more tangible)
- Add electrical layer
- Add electronic control layer
- Add communication layer
- Integrate with dynamics and safety
- Each session builds on previous knowledge

### **Theory/Practice Ratio:**
- **90% Theory** - Graduate-level engineering course focusing on principles
- **10% Applied** - Numerical problems, case studies, assignments, projects
- Practical work through assignments and projects (not hands-on lab)

### **Day-wise Distribution:**
- 20 sessions spread across semester
- Typically 2 sessions per week = 10 weeks
- Or 1 session per week = 20 weeks (depending on program schedule)

## 5. Assessment

### **Assessment Types:**

1. **Quiz (EC-1):** 10% weight
   - Online format
   - 1 week duration
   - Tests foundational understanding

2. **Assignment:** 20% weight
   - Online format
   - 10 days duration
   - Application of concepts, numerical problems, case analysis

3. **Mid-Semester Test (EC-2):** 30% weight
   - Closed book
   - 2 hours duration
   - Covers Sessions 1-8
   - Tests understanding and problem-solving

4. **Comprehensive Exam (EC-3):** 40% weight
   - Open book
   - 2.5 hours duration
   - Covers all sessions
   - Tests application, analysis, and integration

### **Passing Criteria:**

- **Overall:** 40% minimum to pass
- **Best Practice:** 60%+ for good understanding
- No minimum per component requirement

### **Retake Policy:**
- Make-up tests available for genuine exigencies
- Follow BITS Pilani WIL division policies
