## 🎓 Framework Philosophy

### **What Makes These Professional:**

#### **1. Teacher-Led Narrative Structure**
```
Traditional Lecture:
"Today we will discuss forces on vehicles. [Lists topics]"

Teacher-Led Framework:
"Let me ask you something. When you press the accelerator, what's happening?
Most people say 'the engine speeds up.' That's not wrong, but incomplete.
Today, I want to share a different way of thinking..."
```

#### **2. Technical Accuracy + Accessibility**
- All formulas are rigorous and correct
- Every equation is explained in plain language
- Real examples with actual vehicle data
- Professional tone maintained throughout

#### **3. Progressive Confidence Building**
- Start with "you already know this"
- Build complexity gradually
- Celebrate mastery at each step
- Connect to real-world applications

#### **4. Balanced Storytelling**
- **NOT** fictional narratives or entertainment
- **YES** structured learning journeys
- Story arc = pedagogical progression
- Content drives delivery, not vice versa

## 📋 Framework Structure Reference

### **5-Part Story Arc (For Every Session):**

| Part | Slides | Time | Purpose |
|------|--------|------|---------|
| **SETUP** | 1-5 | 8-10 min | Hook, connect, establish relevance |
| **TRIGGER** | 6-7 | 5 min | Present challenge/question |
| **RISING ACTION** | 8-24 | 50-55 min | Build understanding systematically (3-4 parts) |
| **CLIMAX** | 25-27 | 10-12 min | Integrate concepts, solve comprehensively |
| **RESOLUTION** | 28-30 | 10 min | Summarize, connect to next, assign work |
| **Q&A** | - | 30 min | Discussion and clarification |

### **Why This Structure Works:**

✅ **Setup** = Students know where they're going
✅ **Trigger** = Motivation to learn (real problem to solve)
✅ **Rising Action** = Progressive learning (simple → complex)
✅ **Climax** = Mastery moment (aha! I can do this)
✅ **Resolution** = Confidence + next steps clear

**Result:** Engaged students who understand, remember, and can apply.

## 🎨 PPT Design Principles

### **Professional Engineering Aesthetic:**

#### **Color Scheme:**
- **Primary:** Blues (#4472C4) and grays (#808080)
- **Accent:** Orange (#FF6600) for highlighting key points
- **Background:** Clean white or light gray
- **Text:** Dark gray (#333333) - not pure black

#### **Typography:**
- **Headers:** Sans-serif (Calibri, Arial, Segoe UI)
- **Body:** Sans-serif for readability
- **Equations:** Large, clear font (18-24 pt minimum)
- **Code/Units:** Monospace where appropriate

#### **Layouts:**
- **Title slides:** Minimal text, strong visual
- **Content slides:** 40% visual, 60% white space
- **Calculation slides:** Step-by-step, clearly numbered
- **Summary slides:** Clean lists or flowcharts

#### **Visuals:**
- High-quality engineering diagrams
- Clear free-body diagrams with labeled forces
- Professional photographs (components, vehicles)
- Graphs with labeled axes and units
