**Question 10** [E]
**Chapter:** 5 | **Week:** 3 | **Topic:** Fuel Delivery

What is the primary advantage of Gasoline Direct Injection (GDI) over Multi-Point Fuel Injection (MPFI)?

A) Lower fuel system pressure, reducing component stress
B) More precise fuel metering and improved thermal efficiency through stratified charge capability
C) Simpler injector design with fewer moving parts
D) No requirement for a high-pressure fuel pump

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** GDI injects fuel directly into the combustion chamber at very high pressure (200+ bar), allowing precise control of spray pattern and timing. **Question 11** [H]
**Chapter:** 5 | **Week:** 3 | **Topic:** Ignition System Diagnostics

A scan tool shows cylinder 3 misfiring. The technician swaps the ignition coil from cylinder 3 to cylinder 1. The misfire now follows to cylinder 1. What does this confirm?

A) The spark plug on cylinder 3 is faulty
B) The ignition coil is faulty
C) The fuel injector on cylinder 3 is blocked
D) The PCM driver circuit for cylinder 3 is faulty

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The swap test is a fundamental diagnostic technique. If the misfire moves with the swapped component, that component is faulty. The coil was moved from cyl 3 to cyl 1, and the misfire moved from cyl 3 to cyl 1 — confirming the coil is the cause. If the misfire had stayed on cyl 3, the coil would have been eliminated and the plug, injector, or wiring for that cylinder investigated. Covered Day 15.

**Question 13** [M]
**Chapter:** 6 | **Week:** 3 | **Topic:** Diesel Systems

What is the primary function of the diesel Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) system?

A) To increase engine power output
B) To reduce NOx emissions by lowering combustion temperature
C) To improve fuel economy at highway speeds
D) To reduce particulate matter (soot) emissions

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** EGR recirculates a portion of exhaust gas back into the intake. The inert exhaust gas dilutes the fresh charge, lowering peak combustion temperatures. Since NOx formation is highly temperature-dependent, this reduces NOx emissions significantly. However, EGR can increase soot production — there is a NOx-soot trade-off. Covered Day 13.

**Question 14** [M]

A common rail diesel fuel system operates at approximately what injection pressure?

B) 30-50 bar

D) 1600-2500 bar

**Correct Answer:** D
**Explanation:** Modern common rail diesel systems operate at extremely high pressures — typically 1600 to 2500 bar (some reach 2700 bar). **Question 18** [M]

B) 857 RPM

**Question 19** [H]
**Chapter:** 7 | **Week:** 6 | **Topic:** Automatic Transmission

A customer reports their automatic transmission "flares" between 2nd and 3rd gear (engine RPM rises briefly before the next gear engages). What is the most likely cause?

A) Low transmission fluid level or worn clutch packs causing delayed engagement
B) A faulty engine coolant temperature sensor
C) Worn brake pads causing ABS intervention
D) A slipping alternator drive belt

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** "Flare" in an automatic transmission means the engine RPM rises between gears because the next gear's clutch pack has not fully engaged before the current gear releases. The momentary rise in RPM indicates a loss of torque transfer. Covered Day 28.

**Question 20** [E]
**Chapter:** 12 | **Week:** 6 | **Topic:** Starting System

What is the function of the starter motor solenoid?

A) To regulate the charging voltage from the alternator
B) To engage the starter pinion with the flywheel ring gear and close the main power circuit
C) To prevent engine cranking if the transmission is in gear
D) To protect the battery from overcurrent during starting

**Correct Answer:** B

**Question 21** [M]
**Chapter:** 12 | **Week:** 6 | **Topic:** Charging System

An alternator output test shows 13.2V at the battery terminals with the engine at 2000 RPM and all electrical loads switched off. The specification calls for 13.8-14.4V. What is the most likely cause?

A) Normal operation — 13.2V is within specification
B) A faulty voltage regulator or worn brushes causing undercharging
C) A short circuit in the vehicle wiring

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** A healthy alternator should produce 13.8-14.4V under these conditions. At 13.2V, the alternator is undercharging — the battery will gradually discharge, especially with loads active. **Question 22** [M]
**Chapter:** 7 | **Week:** 6 | **Topic:** Driveline

What is the purpose of a Constant Velocity (CV) joint in a front-wheel-drive vehicle?

A) To allow the driveshaft to transmit power at varying angles while the front wheels steer and the suspension moves
B) To disconnect the engine from the wheels during braking
C) To reduce engine vibration transmitted to the cabin
D) To lock the differential during cornering

**Correct Answer:** A
**Explanation:** CV joints allow power transmission through changing angles as the front wheels steer (up to 45+ degrees) and the suspension compresses/extends. Unlike simple universal joints, CV joints maintain constant rotational velocity regardless of angle, preventing vibration. Each front driveshaft (half-shaft) typically has an inner CV joint (plunge-type, handles length changes) and an outer CV joint (fixed, handles steering angles). Covered Day 27.

In a Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV), what component converts the high-voltage DC from the battery pack to AC for the drive motor?

**Question 24** [M]
**Chapter:** 8 | **Week:** 7 | **Topic:** EV Safety

Before performing any work on a high-voltage electric vehicle system, what is the FIRST step after immobilizing the vehicle?

A) Disconnect the 12V auxiliary battery
B) Remove the HV service disconnect (manual service disconnect/MSD) and verify zero voltage
C) Drain the coolant from the battery cooling circuit

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The critical first step is to de-energize the HV system by removing the Manual Service Disconnect (MSD) plug, then waiting the prescribed time for capacitor discharge (typically 5-10 minutes), and then verifying zero voltage with a CAT III rated multimeter at the HV terminals. The 12V battery disconnection may come before or as part of the shutdown procedure depending on the manufacturer, but the MSD removal is the step that physically breaks the HV circuit. Covered Day 31.

**Question 25** [H]
**Chapter:** 8 | **Week:** 7 | **Topic:** Hybrid Systems

In a parallel hybrid vehicle, the electric motor and internal combustion engine can both drive the wheels simultaneously. At what driving condition does this combined operation typically occur?

A) During highway cruising at constant speed
B) During hard acceleration or hill climbing when maximum torque is demanded
C) During low-speed parking manoeuvres
D) During engine braking on a downhill grade

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** In a parallel hybrid, combined operation (ICE + electric motor) occurs when the driver demands more torque than either power source can provide alone — typically hard acceleration, overtaking, or climbing steep grades. At cruising speed, the ICE alone is often sufficient. At low speeds or in traffic, the electric motor may operate alone. During deceleration, regenerative braking recovers energy. This "torque fill" strategy gives the driver seamless power delivery. Covered Day 31.

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The EU F-Gas Regulation mandated the transition because R-1234yf has a GWP of just 4, compared to R-134a's GWP of 1430. **Question 29** [M]

**Question 30** [H]

**Question 31** [E]
**Chapter:** 9 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Lubrication

What does the oil viscosity rating "5W-30" mean?

A) The oil weighs 5 kilograms and is suitable up to 30 degrees Celsius
B) The "5W" indicates winter cold-flow viscosity (thin when cold), and "30" indicates viscosity at operating temperature (100 degrees C)
C) The oil is a blend of 5% synthetic and 30% mineral base
D) The oil should be changed every 5,000 km or 30 days

**Explanation:** In the SAE viscosity grading system, "5W" means the oil meets the viscosity requirements for "Winter" grade 5 — it flows easily at low temperatures (down to -30 degrees C). **Question 32** [M]
**Chapter:** 9 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Oil System

An oil pressure warning light comes on at idle but goes off when the engine RPM increases above 1500. What is the most likely cause?

A) A faulty oil pressure sensor giving intermittent readings
B) Worn engine bearings causing excessive clearance and low oil pressure at low RPM
C) The wrong viscosity oil installed (too thick)
D) A clogged oil filter

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Worn main and rod bearings increase the clearance between the bearing surface and the crankshaft journal. At low RPM (idle), the oil pump cannot maintain sufficient pressure through these enlarged gaps. At higher RPM, the increased pump speed generates enough flow to raise pressure above the warning threshold. This is a serious engine wear indicator requiring prompt investigation. A clogged filter would cause low pressure at all speeds. Covered Day 16.

**Question 33** [E]
**Chapter:** 10 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Cooling System

What is the primary function of the thermostat in an engine cooling system?

A) To measure and display the coolant temperature on the dashboard
B) To regulate coolant flow to maintain the engine at optimal operating temperature by opening and closing a valve
C) To pressurize the cooling system to raise the boiling point
D) To mix the coolant concentrate with water at the correct ratio

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The thermostat is a wax-pellet valve that remains closed when the engine is cold, allowing rapid warm-up by preventing coolant flow to the radiator. Once coolant reaches the thermostat's rated opening temperature (typically 82-95 degrees C), the wax expands, the valve opens, and coolant flows through the radiator for cooling. It modulates to maintain optimal temperature. Covered Day 17.

**Question 34** [H]
**Chapter:** 10 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Cooling Diagnostics

A customer reports their engine overheats in heavy traffic but runs at normal temperature on the motorway. The cooling fans are not activating when the temperature rises. What is the most likely root cause?

A) A stuck-open thermostat
B) A faulty coolant temperature sensor or fan relay preventing the ECU from activating the electric cooling fans
C) A leaking head gasket
D) An overfilled coolant expansion tank

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** In traffic, there is no ram air through the radiator — the electric fans must provide airflow. On the motorway, vehicle speed forces air through the radiator, so overheating doesn't occur. If the fans don't activate, the most common causes are: faulty coolant temperature sensor (ECU doesn't know coolant is hot), blown fan relay, or a wiring fault. A stuck-open thermostat would cause slow warm-up but not overheating. Covered Day 17.

**Question 35** [E]
**Chapter:** 11 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Exhaust System

What is the primary function of a catalytic converter?

A) To reduce exhaust noise
B) To convert harmful exhaust gases (CO, HC, NOx) into less harmful substances (CO2, H2O, N2) through chemical reactions
C) To filter particulate matter from diesel exhaust
D) To increase exhaust backpressure for better engine performance

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The three-way catalytic converter uses precious metal catalysts (platinum, palladium, rhodium) to convert: carbon monoxide (CO) to carbon dioxide (CO2), unburned hydrocarbons (HC) to water (H2O) and CO2, and nitrogen oxides (NOx) to nitrogen (N2) and oxygen (O2). **Question 36** [M]
**Chapter:** 11 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Emissions

What does a Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) do, and what is "regeneration"?

A) It filters fuel impurities; regeneration is the process of replacing the filter element
B) It traps soot particles from diesel exhaust; regeneration is the process of burning off accumulated soot at high temperatures (600+ degrees C)
C) It removes water from diesel fuel; regeneration occurs when the engine is switched off
D) It reduces NOx emissions; regeneration is the process of injecting AdBlue

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The DPF physically traps soot (particulate matter) in a honeycomb ceramic or silicon carbide structure. As soot accumulates, exhaust backpressure increases. Regeneration raises the DPF temperature to 600+ degrees C (via post-injection, exhaust throttle, or intake throttle) to burn off the soot, converting it to CO2. Passive regeneration occurs naturally during highway driving; active regeneration is ECU-initiated. Covered Day 18.

**Question 37** [M]
**Chapter:** 11 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Forced Induction

What is the purpose of an intercooler in a turbocharged engine?

A) To cool the engine oil after it passes through the turbocharger
B) To cool the compressed intake air after the turbocharger, increasing its density for more power and reducing the risk of detonation
C) To cool the exhaust gases before they enter the catalytic converter
D) To provide additional cooling for the engine coolant

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Compressing air in the turbocharger heats it significantly (up to 150+ degrees C). Hot air is less dense — so without cooling, the turbo's volumetric benefit is reduced. The intercooler (charge air cooler) reduces intake air temperature, increasing density (more oxygen per volume for combustion) and reducing the risk of knock/detonation in gasoline engines. **Question 38** [H]
**Chapter:** 11 | **Week:** 4 | **Topic:** Turbocharger Diagnostics

A turbocharged diesel vehicle produces excessive black smoke under acceleration and has reduced power. Boost pressure measured at the intake manifold reads 0.4 bar when the specification calls for 1.2 bar. What are the two most likely causes?

A) Clogged cabin air filter and faulty radio antenna
B) Wastegate stuck open or a split/disconnected boost pressure hose (charge pipe leak)
C) Worn brake pads and low tyre pressure
D) Faulty alternator and weak battery

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Low boost pressure under load narrows the cause to the turbo/boost system: (1) a wastegate stuck open would dump exhaust gas past the turbine, preventing it from spinning fast enough; (2) a split or disconnected charge pipe leaks compressed air before it reaches the cylinders. **Question 39** [E]
**Chapter:** 13 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** Suspension

What is the function of a shock absorber (damper) in the suspension system?

A) To support the weight of the vehicle
B) To control the rate of spring oscillation and prevent continuous bouncing after a bump
C) To maintain the correct ride height
D) To steer the vehicle

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Springs absorb bumps but would continue oscillating (bouncing) without a damper. The shock absorber (damper) converts kinetic energy from spring oscillation into heat energy by forcing hydraulic fluid through restrictive valves. This controls the rate of compression and rebound, providing a single, controlled response to road irregularities. Covered Day 22.

**Question 40** [M]
**Chapter:** 13 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** Steering

What is the primary advantage of Electric Power Steering (EPS) over Hydraulic Power Steering (HPS)?

A) EPS provides more steering feel at highway speeds
B) EPS is more energy-efficient because it only consumes power when steering assistance is needed, whereas HPS runs a pump continuously
C) EPS can generate higher steering forces than hydraulic systems
D) EPS requires no maintenance over the vehicle's lifetime

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** A hydraulic power steering pump runs continuously off the engine via a belt, consuming power even when driving straight. An EPS motor only draws electrical energy when the driver turns the wheel, saving fuel (approximately 2-3% improvement in fuel economy). EPS also allows variable assist — more help at low speed for parking, less at high speed for better road feel — and enables integration with ADAS features like lane-keep assist. Covered Day 22.

**Question 41** [E]
**Chapter:** 14 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** Brakes

In a hydraulic disc brake system, what component squeezes the brake pads against the rotor?

A) The master cylinder
B) The brake caliper
C) The brake booster
D) The ABS modulator

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The brake caliper straddles the brake rotor (disc). When hydraulic pressure from the master cylinder enters the caliper, it pushes the piston(s) outward, clamping the brake pads against both sides of the spinning rotor. This friction converts kinetic energy to heat, slowing the wheel. Covered Day 23.

**Question 42** [M]
**Chapter:** 14 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** ABS

How does ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) prevent wheel lock-up during hard braking?

A) It increases hydraulic pressure to all wheels simultaneously
B) It rapidly modulates (increases and decreases) hydraulic pressure to individual wheels using solenoid valves when wheel speed sensors detect a wheel about to lock
C) It disconnects the brake pedal from the master cylinder
D) It applies the parking brake electronically

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** ABS monitors individual wheel speeds via tone rings and speed sensors. When a wheel decelerates faster than the vehicle (indicating imminent lock-up), the ABS modulator uses solenoid valves to briefly release, hold, and reapply hydraulic pressure to that specific wheel — cycling up to 15 times per second. This keeps the tyre in the peak friction zone (10-20% slip) rather than fully locked (100% slip), maintaining steering control. Covered Day 24.

**Question 43** [H]
**Chapter:** 14 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** Brake Diagnostics

A customer reports a pulsating brake pedal when braking at moderate speed on a straight road. No warning lights are illuminated. What is the most likely cause?

A) ABS activating due to a faulty wheel speed sensor
B) Brake disc thickness variation (DTV) or warped rotors causing uneven pad contact
C) Air in the hydraulic brake lines
D) Worn brake pad material

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Brake pedal pulsation during normal (non-ABS) braking almost always indicates Disc Thickness Variation (DTV) — uneven thickness around the rotor circumference. As the thick and thin spots rotate through the caliper, the pads push back against the piston cyclically, and this pulsation feeds back through the hydraulic system to the pedal. Often incorrectly called "warped rotors." ABS pulsation (answer A) would trigger the ABS light or occur only during hard braking. Air in lines (C) causes a spongy pedal, not pulsation. Covered Day 23.

What does the tyre marking "205/55 R16 91V" indicate?

B) 205 km/h max speed, 55 psi pressure, R for "reinforced," 16 layers, 91 kg load, V for "vehicle"

**Question 45** [M]
**Chapter:** 13 | **Week:** 5 | **Topic:** Vehicle Dynamics

What is "understeer" and what causes it?

A) The rear of the vehicle slides outward in a corner; caused by excessive rear tyre grip
B) The front tyres lose grip before the rear tyres in a corner, causing the vehicle to follow a wider arc than the driver intends; caused by exceeding front tyre grip limits
C) The vehicle brakes harder than intended; caused by a faulty brake booster
D) The steering wheel returns to centre too quickly; caused by excessive caster angle

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Understeer occurs when the front tyres exceed their lateral grip capacity before the rear tyres. The car "pushes" wide — the driver turns the steering wheel but the car doesn't turn as sharply as intended. Most production cars are designed to understeer rather than oversteer because it is a more intuitive condition for average drivers to manage. Covered Day 21.

**Question 49** [H]

A technician is presented with a vehicle where the ABS warning light, traction control light, and ESC light are all illuminated simultaneously. The customer also reports the speedometer reads zero while driving. What is the single most likely root cause?

#### Slide 19: Time Management
**Visual:** Clock graphic divided into segments showing a 90-minute strategy

**Instructor Narration:**
> "Let's talk strategy. The certification exam is 50 questions in 90 minutes. That's 1 minute and 48 seconds per question. Here's how to use that time:
>
> First pass (60 minutes): Go through all 50 questions. Answer everything you know immediately — don't overthink. If a question takes more than 30 seconds, mark it and move on.
> Second pass (20 minutes): Return to marked questions. Now take your time. Eliminate wrong answers. Apply reasoning.
> Final pass (10 minutes): Review your answer sheet. Make sure every question has an answer. Check for any obvious errors. Don't change answers unless you're certain — your first instinct is usually right."

**PPT Content:**
```
TIME MANAGEMENT — 90 MINUTES, 50 QUESTIONS

FIRST PASS (60 min): Answer what you KNOW
  — Read question, select answer, move on
  — If unsure after 30 seconds: MARK IT, pick best guess, move on
  — Goal: Answer 35-40 questions confidently

SECOND PASS (20 min): Tackle MARKED questions
  — Eliminate 2 obviously wrong answers
  — Choose between remaining 2 with reasoning
  — No blank answers — always guess if unsure

FINAL PASS (10 min): REVIEW
  — Check every question has an answer
  — Re-read any you feel uncertain about
  — Trust your first instinct — don't second-guess
```

#### Slide 20: Elimination Technique
**Visual:** Example question with options A-D, two options crossed out in red, two remaining highlighted

**Instructor Narration:**
> "The elimination technique is your secret weapon. Most MCQ questions have four options. Usually, two of them are clearly wrong if you think about it — they're from a completely different system, or they contradict basic physics. Cross them out mentally. Now you have a 50/50 chance instead of 25%. Even if you're completely guessing at this point, you've doubled your odds.
>
> Let me show you. Look at this question about brake pulsation. Option C says 'air in the lines' — but air causes a spongy pedal, not pulsation. Eliminated. Option D says 'worn pad material' — worn pads cause noise and poor braking, not pulsation. Eliminated. Now you're choosing between A and B with good reasoning."

**PPT Content:**
```
ELIMINATION TECHNIQUE

Step 1: Read all four options
Step 2: Cross out answers that are obviously wrong
  — From the wrong system?  ELIMINATE
  — Contradicts basic physics? ELIMINATE
  — Uses a term you don't recognise from the course? SUSPICIOUS

Step 3: Choose between remaining options
  — Which fits the symptom/scenario better?
  — Which is more specific and technically correct?

RESULT: Even if you guess, you go from 25% to 50% probability

NEVER LEAVE A QUESTION BLANK
A wrong answer and a blank answer score the same: zero
But a guess might be right!
```

## Instructor Notes

**Before the session:**
- Print 50-question mock exam papers (one per learner) — ensure exam is printed clearly with no formatting errors
- Print answer sheets (separate from question papers) — simple grid: Q1-Q50 with A/B/C/D bubbles
- Prepare the room in exam configuration: individual desks, adequate spacing, clock visible
- Have a pre-prepared answer key slide deck ready for the review section
- Prepare score summary template handouts
- Prepare certification exam registration information packets
- Have tissues, water, and a calm demeanour ready — some learners will be anxious

**During the mock exam (90 minutes):**
- Maintain exam conditions strictly — this prepares learners for the real thing
- Give time warnings at 60 minutes (30 remaining), 80 minutes (10 remaining), and 88 minutes (2 remaining)
- Do not answer content questions — only clarify exam procedure
- Note any questions that cause visible confusion across the class — address these first in review

**During the review (45 minutes):**
- Be encouraging but honest — celebrate correct answers, explain incorrect ones without judgment
- Identify the 5-7 most-missed questions and spend extra time on these
- Use the review as a teaching moment — not just "the answer is B" but WHY B is correct
- Track class-level performance per chapter group on the board

**During the wrap-up (20 minutes):**
- This is an emotional moment for many learners — acknowledge the significance
- Be specific in your praise — reference individual moments of growth you witnessed
- Ensure every learner leaves with: their scored mock exam, their personal revision plan, and certification exam registration information
- If offering Weeks 9-11, take sign-up commitments today while motivation is high

**Post-session:**
- Compile class performance data by chapter group
- Identify any learners who may need individual follow-up
- Prepare Week 9-11 content focus based on class weakness areas
- File mock exam results for program quality tracking
