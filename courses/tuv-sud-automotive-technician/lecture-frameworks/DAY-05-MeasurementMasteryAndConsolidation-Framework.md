### **PRACTICAL PART 1: Timed Measurement Challenge** (Slides 4-9, ~60 minutes)

#### Slide 4: Station 1 — Micrometer
**Visual:** Photo of station setup — micrometer, 3 components, worksheet

**Station Setup:**
- Outside micrometer 0-25mm (calibrated, zeroed)
- Component A: Brake disc (measure thickness at 3 points)
- Component B: Piston pin (measure diameter)
- Component C: Valve stem (measure diameter)
- Worksheet with specification values and tolerance columns

**Pass criteria:** All 5 readings within ±0.01 mm of known value. Correct technique observed (ratchet stop used, clean surfaces, proper grip).

---

#### Slide 5: Station 2 — Vernier Caliper
**Visual:** Station setup — caliper, 3 measurement tasks

**Station Setup:**
- Vernier caliper 0-150mm
- Task A: Measure outside diameter of a bearing journal (outside jaws)
- Task B: Measure inside diameter of a bearing cap bore (inside jaws)
- Task C: Measure depth of a recess/counterbore (depth rod)

**Pass criteria:** All 3 readings within ±0.02 mm. Square jaw contact observed.

---

#### Slide 6: Station 3 — Dial Gauge
**Visual:** Dial gauge on magnetic stand positioned on a brake disc on a dummy hub

**Station Setup:**
- Dial gauge with magnetic stand
- Brake disc mounted on a simulated hub (can be rotated by hand)
- Task: Set up gauge, zero it, rotate disc one full turn, record maximum runout
- Specification card: "Maximum lateral runout: 0.10 mm"

**Pass criteria:** Gauge correctly mounted (perpendicular, pre-loaded). Runout reading within ±0.01 mm of instructor's reference measurement. Correct go/no-go verdict.

---

#### Slide 7: Station 4 — Feeler Gauge
**Visual:** 5 simulated gaps with a feeler gauge set

**Station Setup:**
- 5 pairs of precision ground blocks with known gaps:
  - Gap A: 0.15 mm (simulates tight valve clearance)
  - Gap B: 0.25 mm (simulates nominal valve clearance)
  - Gap C: 0.40 mm (simulates worn valve clearance)
  - Gap D: 0.10 mm (simulates spark plug gap)
  - Gap E: 0.30 mm (simulates piston ring end gap)
- Task: Measure each gap using go/no-go method, record blade thickness

**Pass criteria:** 4 of 5 gaps correctly identified within one blade thickness.

#### Slide 8: Station 5 — Torque Challenge
**Visual:** Practice plate with 5 bolts, torque wrench, specification card

**Station Setup:**
- Practice plate (bolted to bench) with 5 fasteners:
  - Bolt 1: M8, torque to 25 Nm
  - Bolt 2: M10, torque to 45 Nm
  - Bolt 3: M10, torque to 65 Nm
  - Bolt 4: M12, torque to 90 Nm
  - Bolt 5: M10, torque to 40 Nm + 90° angle
- Click torque wrench + angle gauge

**Pass criteria:** Correct wrench setting for each bolt. Stopped at click. Bolt 5 angle within ±5°. Wrench returned to lowest setting after use.

### **DEVELOPMENT: Unit Conversions** (Slides 10-15, ~30 minutes)

#### Slide 10: Why Conversions Matter
**Visual:** Two spec sheets side by side — one in metric, one in imperial

**Instructor Narration:**
> "Spec sheets come from manufacturers worldwide. European cars use metric exclusively. American vehicles mix metric and imperial. Japanese and Korean mostly metric. You need to convert fluently between both systems — especially for torque, pressure, temperature, and length."

**PPT Content:**
```
WHY UNIT CONVERSIONS?

European vehicles: Fully metric
American vehicles: Mixed (metric hardware, imperial specs sometimes)
Workshop tools: May be metric or imperial
Spec sheets: Vary by manufacturer and region

YOU NEED TO CONVERT:
• Torque: Nm ↔ ft-lb
• Pressure: bar ↔ PSI ↔ kPa
• Temperature: °C ↔ °F
• Length: mm ↔ inches
```

---

#### Slide 11: Torque Conversion
**Visual:** Conversion formula with worked examples

**Instructor Narration:**
> "Torque is the most common conversion you'll need. Newton-metres to foot-pounds: multiply by 0.7376. Foot-pounds to Newton-metres: multiply by 1.3558. Quick mental shortcut: Nm times 0.74 gets you close enough for a quick estimate. But always use the exact number for critical fasteners."

**PPT Content:**
```
TORQUE: Nm ↔ ft-lb

Nm → ft-lb: multiply by 0.7376
ft-lb → Nm: multiply by 1.3558

EXAMPLES:
• 25 Nm = 25 × 0.7376 = 18.4 ft-lb
• 110 Nm = 110 × 0.7376 = 81.1 ft-lb
• 80 ft-lb = 80 × 1.3558 = 108.5 Nm

QUICK SHORTCUT: Nm × 0.74 ≈ ft-lb (estimate only)

COMMON VALUES:
| Application | Nm | ft-lb |
|------------|-----|-------|
| Wheel nuts (typical) | 110 | 81 |
| Spark plugs | 25 | 18 |
| Sump plug | 40 | 30 |
| Head bolts (typical) | 60+90° | — |
```

---

#### Slide 12: Pressure Conversion
**Visual:** Triangle showing bar-PSI-kPa relationships

**PPT Content:**
```
PRESSURE: bar ↔ PSI ↔ kPa

1 bar = 14.504 PSI = 100 kPa

CONVERSIONS:
bar → PSI: multiply by 14.5
PSI → bar: divide by 14.5
bar → kPa: multiply by 100

COMMON VALUES:
| Application | bar | PSI | kPa |
|------------|-----|-----|-----|
| Tyre pressure (typical) | 2.3 | 33 | 230 |
| Fuel rail (gasoline DI) | 200 | 2,900 | 20,000 |
| Oil pressure (idle) | 1.0 | 14.5 | 100 |
| AC system high side | 15 | 218 | 1,500 |
| Cooling system cap | 1.2 | 17.4 | 120 |
```

---

#### Slide 13: Temperature Conversion
**Visual:** Thermometer with dual scales and key reference points

**PPT Content:**
```
TEMPERATURE: °C ↔ °F

°C → °F: multiply by 1.8, then add 32
°F → °C: subtract 32, then divide by 1.8

REFERENCE POINTS:
| Description | °C | °F |
|------------|-----|-----|
| Water freezes | 0 | 32 |
| Body temperature | 37 | 98.6 |
| Water boils | 100 | 212 |
| Engine thermostat opens | 85-95 | 185-203 |
| Exhaust manifold | 600+ | 1,100+ |
| Catalytic converter | 400-800 | 750-1,470 |

QUICK SHORTCUT: Double °C and add 30 ≈ °F (rough estimate)
```

---

#### Slide 14: Length Conversion
**Visual:** Ruler showing mm and inches side by side

**PPT Content:**
```
LENGTH: mm ↔ inches

1 inch = 25.4 mm exactly
1 mm = 0.03937 inches

CONVERSIONS:
mm → inches: divide by 25.4
inches → mm: multiply by 25.4

COMMON VALUES:
| Description | mm | inches |
|------------|-----|--------|
| Brake pad minimum | 2.0 | 0.079 |
| Disc minimum thickness | 22.0 | 0.866 |
| Tread depth minimum | 1.6 | 0.063 |
| Valve clearance (typical) | 0.25 | 0.010 |
| Piston clearance | 0.03 | 0.001 |

QUICK SHORTCUT: 1 inch ≈ 25 mm (close enough for estimates)
```

#### Slide 20: Quiz Questions

**Q1.** How many major systems make up a modern vehicle?
A) 4 B) 6 C) 8 D) 10
**Answer: C** — Engine, transmission, electrical, suspension, steering, brakes, HVAC, body

**Q2.** What is the correct sequence for operating a 2-post lift after positioning the arms?
A) Raise to full height → engage locks → push test
B) Raise 10 cm → check pad contact → raise to height → engage locks → push test
C) Engage locks → raise to height → push test
D) Raise to height → push test → engage locks
**Answer: B** — The 10 cm check is the critical safety step before full raise

**Q3.** Which fire extinguisher type is correct for an electrical panel fire?
A) Water B) Foam C) CO₂ D) Wet chemical
**Answer: C** — CO₂ leaves no residue and is non-conductive

**Q4.** What happens when IAT (green) coolant is mixed with OAT (orange) coolant?
A) Nothing — they are compatible B) The colour changes but it works fine
C) Gel/sludge forms that clogs passages D) The mixture becomes more effective
**Answer: C** — Gel formation clogs heater core and radiator passages

**Q5.** What is the resolution of a standard outside micrometer?
A) 0.1 mm B) 0.05 mm C) 0.02 mm D) 0.01 mm
**Answer: D** — The thimble has 50 divisions, each representing 0.01 mm

**Q6.** A bolt marked "10.9" on its head has a tensile strength of approximately:
A) 109 MPa B) 400 MPa C) 800 MPa D) 1,040 MPa
**Answer: D** — First digit × 100 = 10 × 100 = 1,000 MPa (actual: 1,040)

**Q7.** Which section of an SDS contains first aid measures?
A) Section 2 B) Section 4 C) Section 7 D) Section 11
**Answer: B** — Section 4 covers first aid for eye, skin, inhalation, ingestion

**Q8.** What should you ALWAYS do after using a click torque wrench?
A) Clean it with solvent B) Return it to the lowest setting
C) Calibrate it D) Store it at the last used setting
**Answer: B** — Leaving it set compresses the spring and loses calibration

**Q9.** DOT 5 brake fluid uses which base?
A) Glycol B) Mineral oil C) Silicone D) Synthetic ester
**Answer: C** — DOT 5 is silicone-based and NOT compatible with DOT 3/4/5.1

**Q10.** In a risk assessment matrix, Risk = ?
A) Severity + Likelihood B) Severity × Likelihood
C) Severity ÷ Likelihood D) Severity − Likelihood
**Answer: B** — Risk score is the product of likelihood and severity ratings
