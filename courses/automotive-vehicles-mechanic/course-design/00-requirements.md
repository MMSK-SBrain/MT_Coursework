# Course Requirements: Automotive Mechanic Specialization

**Created:** 2026-01-11
**Status:** Complete - Ready for Learning Outcomes Development
**Course Code:** AMS
**Duration:** 30 Days (6 Weeks)

---

## 1. Course Context

- **Topic:** Automotive Mechanic Specialization (Track A: Mechanical Maintenance)
- **Industry/Domain:** Automotive Service & Repair
- **Duration (Days):** 30 days (180 hours: 3 hours theory + 3 hours practical daily)
- **Description:** Comprehensive vocational training program for automotive mechanical technicians covering precision measurement, engine systems, advanced technologies (VVT, diesel, turbo), transmission systems, chassis/suspension/brakes, and integrated diagnostics.

## 2. Target Audience

- **Primary Learners:** Entry-level automotive technicians, automotive service apprentices
- **Experience Level:** Basic automotive knowledge (completion of 30-day foundation program assumed)
- **Prerequisites:**
  - Completed 30-day automotive foundation course
  - Basic vehicle systems knowledge
  - Basic hand tool proficiency
  - Safety awareness in workshop environment
- **Job Role:** Automotive Service Technician - Mechanical Maintenance (NSQF Level 5)

## 3. Learning Outcomes

### What learners will DO:

1. **Perform precision measurements** on engine components using micrometers, calipers, dial indicators, and bore gauges to determine wear and serviceability
2. **Diagnose and repair mechanical systems** including engines, transmissions, fuel systems, cooling systems, and lubrication systems
3. **Service advanced engine technologies** including VVT systems, diesel engines, and turbocharged powertrains
4. **Overhaul and service transmissions** including manual, automatic (AMT/CVT), and dual-clutch (DSG/DCT) systems
5. **Service chassis systems** including suspension, steering, brakes, and ABS/EBD systems
6. **Perform wheel alignment** and diagnose tire wear patterns
7. **Diagnose emission control systems** (BS4/BS6) and resolve check engine light issues
8. **Execute preventive maintenance services** following manufacturer specifications
9. **Apply systematic diagnostic methodology** to complex mechanical problems
10. **Communicate professionally** with customers regarding repair recommendations and estimates

### Critical Skills:

- Precision measurement and interpretation (±0.01mm accuracy)
- Proper torque procedures and fastener service
- Engine disassembly, inspection, and assembly
- Transmission service and fluid procedures
- Brake system overhaul and bleeding
- Wheel alignment execution
- Scan tool operation for mechanical diagnostics
- Systematic troubleshooting methodology
- Professional customer communication
- Workshop safety and tool management

### Nice-to-have:

- Advanced diagnostic techniques beyond scan tools
- Performance tuning knowledge
- Hybrid/electric vehicle awareness
- Customer service excellence

## 4. Content Structure

### Main Modules (6 Weeks):

**Week 1: Foundations of Precision Work**
- Precision measurement and tool mastery
- Fasteners, torque, and thread repair
- Engine disassembly and inspection
- Engine wear measurement and assessment
- Engine assembly preparation

**Week 2: Advanced Engine Technologies**
- Variable Valve Timing (VVT) systems
- Diesel engine fundamentals and service
- Diesel engine overhaul procedures
- Turbocharger systems and operation
- Turbo diagnosis and installation

**Week 3: Engine Support & Emissions Systems**
- Fuel system service and diagnosis
- Cooling system service and diagnosis
- Lubrication system service
- Emission control systems (BS4 & BS6)
- Emission system diagnosis and repair

**Week 4: Transmission & Drivetrain Systems**
- Manual transmission overhaul
- Clutch system service and replacement
- Automatic transmission basics (AMT/CVT)
- DSG/DCT transmission service
- Transmission diagnosis and fluid service

**Week 5: Chassis, Suspension & Brakes**
- Suspension system service
- Wheel alignment theory and practice
- Steering system service
- Hydraulic brake system overhaul
- ABS/EBD systems and diagnosis

**Week 6: Integration, Diagnostics & Capstone**
- Mechanical diagnostics methodology
- Preventive maintenance execution
- Capstone project (2-day real vehicle service)
- Final assessment and certification

### Sequencing:

- **Logical progression:** Tools → Engine internals → Engine systems → Powertrain → Chassis → Integration
- **Skill building:** Each week builds on previous week's competencies
- **Safety first:** Safety protocols integrated throughout, emphasized from Day 1
- **Theory → Practice:** Each day: 3 hours theory morning, 3 hours hands-on afternoon

### Theory/Practice Ratio:

- **50/50 split:** 3 hours theory + 3 hours practical daily
- **Actually 70% practical time** when considering hands-on nature of workshop training
- Real equipment usage emphasized over simulations
- Every theoretical concept has immediate practical application same day

## 5. Assessment

### Types:

1. **Daily Pass Criteria:** Each practical session has specific pass criteria (e.g., "9 out of 10 measurements within ±0.01mm")
2. **Weekly Assessments:**
   - Practical Exam (hands-on skills demonstration)
   - Skills Test (specific procedures under observation)
   - Written Test (theory, specifications, troubleshooting)
3. **Capstone Project (Days 58-59):**
   - Complete vehicle diagnosis and repair
   - Customer presentation
   - Professional documentation
4. **Final Comprehensive Assessment (Day 60):**
   - Written exam: 190 questions (70% pass mark)
   - Practical exam: 3 stations with 180 points total (70% pass mark)

### Passing Criteria:

- **Daily practicals:** Meet specified pass criteria for each task
- **Weekly assessments:** 70% minimum across all three components
- **Capstone project:** Successful completion of all deliverables with professional quality
- **Final assessment:** 70% on both written (133/190) and practical (126/180 points)
- **Overall:** Must pass all weekly assessments + capstone + final to receive certification

### Assessment Focus:

- **Safety:** Non-negotiable - 100% compliance required
- **Accuracy:** Measurements within specifications
- **Procedure:** Following correct sequences and methods
- **Professionalism:** Documentation, customer communication, workplace organization
- **Problem-solving:** Diagnostic methodology and root cause identification

## 6. Constraints

### Equipment:

- **Essential tools:** Micrometers, dial indicators, bore gauges, torque wrenches, scan tools
- **Workshop equipment:** Lifts, engine hoists, transmission jacks, alignment machines, brake lathes
- **Practice engines:** Used engines for disassembly and measurement practice
- **Live vehicles:** Customer vehicles or training vehicles for capstone project
- **Ratio:** Maximum 4 students per vehicle/engine for hands-on work

### Safety:

- **Workshop safety protocols:** Mandatory PPE, lifting procedures, hot surfaces, rotating equipment
- **Fluid handling:** Proper disposal of oil, coolant, brake fluid
- **Pressure systems:** Fuel pressure, brake pressure safety procedures
- **Tool safety:** Torque wrench calibration, measurement tool handling
- **Vehicle safety:** Proper jacking and support, battery disconnection procedures

### Time Constraints:

- **Fixed 30-day duration:** Cannot extend beyond 6 weeks
- **Daily 6-hour delivery:** 3+3 format maintains engagement
- **Capstone realism:** 2-day project simulates real-world time pressure

### Facility Requirements:

- **Well-equipped workshop** with adequate workspace per student
- **Parts availability** for all planned repairs and services
- **Reference materials:** Service manuals, specifications, TSBs
- **Scan tools:** OBD-II capable with live data and bidirectional controls

## 7. Career Outcomes

### Certification:

- **Industry certification:** ASC/Q6808 Automotive Service Technician - Mechanical (NSQF Level 5)
- **Certificate includes:** Competency in all 6 modules with skill verification

### Employment Readiness:

- **Job roles:** Mechanical Technician, Engine Specialist, Transmission Specialist
- **Salary expectations:** ₹18,000 - ₹25,000/month (entry level in India)
- **Growth path:** Senior Technician → Master Technician → Shop Foreman → Service Manager

### Employability Skills:

- Technical competence across all mechanical systems
- Professional customer communication
- Systematic diagnostic thinking
- Quality workmanship and attention to detail
- Workshop safety consciousness
- Continuous learning mindset

## 8. Instructional Design Principles Applied

### Backward Design:
- Started with job requirements (what mechanics actually DO)
- Designed assessments to match real-world performance
- Structured learning experiences to build those specific skills

### Bloom's Taxonomy:
- Targeting **Apply level and higher** throughout (appropriate for vocational)
- Knowledge and Understanding in theory sessions
- Apply, Analyze, and Evaluate in practical sessions
- Create level in capstone project (independent problem-solving)

### Cognitive Load Management:
- 3-5 new concepts per theory session
- Immediate practice reinforces learning same day
- Complex skills broken into sub-skills (e.g., clutch replacement broken into: removal → inspection → installation)
- Worked examples before independent practice

### Spaced Practice & Interleaving:
- Core skills (measurement, torque, diagnosis) revisited across multiple weeks
- Different systems taught in sequence but integrated in Week 6
- Retrieval practice through weekly assessments

### Vocational Training Best Practices:
- **Safety first:** Emphasized from Day 1
- **Tools before systems:** Week 1 establishes foundational tool skills
- **Real equipment prioritized:** Minimal simulation
- **Troubleshooting methodology:** Systematic approach taught and practiced
- **70/30 practice-to-theory ratio** (effective ratio with hands-on time)
