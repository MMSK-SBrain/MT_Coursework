# Learning Outcomes Hierarchy: Automotive Mechanic Specialization

**Created:** 2026-01-11
**Status:** Complete
**Course Code:** AMS
**Duration:** 30 Days (6 Weeks)
**Framework:** CO → MO → SO (Course → Module → Session)

---

## Overview

This document defines the complete learning outcomes hierarchy for the Automotive Mechanic Specialization course using backward design principles. Outcomes are structured in three levels:

- **Course Outcomes (COs)**: High-level competencies achieved after completing the entire 30-day program
- **Module Outcomes (MOs)**: Competencies achieved after completing each week (6 modules)
- **Session Outcomes (SOs)**: Specific objectives for each daily session (30 sessions)

All outcomes are written as observable, measurable behaviors using Bloom's Taxonomy (targeting Apply level and higher for vocational training).

---

## Course Outcomes (COs)

### AMS-CO-1: Precision Measurement and Engine Systems
**ID:** co-1
**Code:** AMS-CO-1
**Category:** Skill
**Bloom's Level:** Apply, Analyze

**Outcome:**
Diagnose, disassemble, measure, and reassemble automotive engines using precision measurement tools (micrometers, dial indicators, bore gauges) to determine component wear and serviceability within ±0.01mm accuracy, following manufacturer specifications and torque procedures.

**Assessment:**
- Demonstrate precision measurement of engine components with 9/10 measurements within specification
- Complete engine disassembly, inspection, and reassembly with proper torque sequences
- Diagnose engine wear patterns and recommend repair/replacement decisions

---

### AMS-CO-2: Advanced Powertrain Technologies
**ID:** co-2
**Code:** AMS-CO-2
**Category:** Skill
**Bloom's Level:** Apply, Analyze

**Outcome:**
Service and diagnose advanced engine technologies including Variable Valve Timing (VVT) systems, diesel engines, turbocharged powertrains, and emission control systems (BS4/BS6), using scan tools and systematic diagnostic methodology to resolve mechanical and emissions issues.

**Assessment:**
- Diagnose VVT system faults using scan tool live data
- Perform diesel engine service including injector testing and timing verification
- Diagnose turbocharger performance issues and execute replacement
- Resolve check engine light issues related to emissions systems

---

### AMS-CO-3: Transmission and Drivetrain Systems
**ID:** co-3
**Code:** AMS-CO-3
**Category:** Skill
**Bloom's Level:** Apply, Analyze

**Outcome:**
Overhaul, service, and diagnose manual transmissions, clutch systems, and automatic transmissions (AMT/CVT/DSG) including fluid service, component inspection, and failure analysis, ensuring proper assembly and operational verification.

**Assessment:**
- Complete manual transmission overhaul with synchronizer inspection
- Replace clutch system with proper alignment and torque procedures
- Perform automatic transmission fluid service and adaptation procedures
- Diagnose transmission shifting issues using scan tool and road test

---

### AMS-CO-4: Chassis Systems and Vehicle Safety
**ID:** co-4
**Code:** AMS-CO-4
**Category:** Skill
**Bloom's Level:** Apply, Analyze, Evaluate

**Outcome:**
Service, diagnose, and repair chassis systems including suspension components, steering systems, hydraulic brakes, and ABS/EBD systems, and perform wheel alignment to manufacturer specifications while diagnosing tire wear patterns.

**Assessment:**

- Diagnose ABS/EBD system faults using scan tool and multimeter

**ID:** co-5
**Code:** AMS-CO-5

## Instructional Design Principles Applied

### 1. Backward Design
- Started with employment requirements (NSQF Level 5 Automotive Service Technician)
- Defined COs based on job competencies
- Created MOs and SOs to build toward COs
- Designed assessments to measure outcomes
- Instruction will be designed to achieve outcomes

### 2. Bloom's Taxonomy
- **Targeting Apply level and higher** (vocational focus)
- Knowledge/Understanding in theory sessions (morning)
- Apply/Analyze in practical sessions (afternoon)
- Evaluate/Create in capstone project
- Progression from lower to higher cognitive levels across 6 weeks

### 3. Cognitive Load Management
- **3-5 outcomes per day** (manageable chunks)
- Immediate practice after theory (same day)
- Complex skills broken into sub-skills (e.g., engine assembly across 3 days)
- Spaced repetition (measurement skills revisited weeks 1, 4, 5, 6)

### 4. Gagné's 9 Events (Session Structure)
Each daily session will follow:
1. Gain attention (demonstration, problem scenario)
2. Inform objectives (tell students the SO for the day)
3. Stimulate recall (connect to previous learning)
4. Present content (theory instruction)
5. Provide guidance (worked examples, demonstrations)
6. Elicit performance (hands-on practice)
7. Provide feedback (instructor coaching during practice)
8. Assess performance (pass criteria check)
9. Enhance retention (summary, preview next day)

### 5. Vocational Training Best Practices
- **Safety emphasized:** Day 1 and throughout (100% compliance required)
- **Tools before systems:** Week 1 establishes measurement and torque fundamentals
- **Real equipment:** All SOs use actual vehicles, engines, and systems
- **70/30 practice-to-theory ratio:** 3 hours theory + 3 hours practical daily
- **Troubleshooting methodology:** Taught Week 6, practiced throughout
- **Progressive complexity:** Simple → Complex → Integration

### 6. Spaced Practice and Interleaving
- **Core skills revisited:** Measurement (Weeks 1, 4, 5, 6), Torque (Weeks 1-6), Diagnosis (Weeks 2-6)
- **System integration:** Individual systems (Weeks 1-5) → Integrated diagnostics (Week 6)
- **Assessment spacing:** Daily, Weekly, Capstone, Final
