# Course Requirements: Inclusive Management for Shop Floor Supervisors

**Created:** 2026-02-13
**Status:** Gathering

## 1. Course Context
- **Topic:** Professional interactions and management of women employees in shop floor
- **Industry/Domain:** Manufacturing (India factory setting)
- **Duration:** 1 day (8 hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM)
- **Description:** Training for male managers to develop respectful, professional workplace behaviors and inclusive management practices

## 2. Target Audience
- **Primary Learners:** Male managers/supervisors in shop floor environment
- **Experience Level:** 2-15 years management experience, all engineers
- **Prerequisites:** None - no prior diversity/inclusion training
- **Age Range:** 28-50 years
- **Current State:** Engineering background, traditional factory environment

## 3. Training Context & Drivers
- **Type:** Reactive training (addressing specific reported issues)
- **Specific Issues Reported:**
  - Rude behavior toward women employees
  - Not offering seats even during period days
  - General lack of politeness and courtesy
  - Female employees have filed complaints
- **Training Status:** Currently voluntary, may become mandatory if successful
- **Goal:** Address immediate behavioral issues and build foundation for inclusive culture

## 4. Learning Outcomes
### What learners will DO differently (Behavioral Changes):
- Be more polite and inclusive to female counterparts
- Make women employees feel welcome and integral part of team
- Demonstrate basic courtesies (offering seats, especially during health needs)
- Show empathy and emotional awareness in daily interactions
- Exercise emotional control in workplace situations

### Critical Must-Fix Behaviors (All are important):
- Eliminate rude behavior toward women employees
- Provide basic workplace courtesies (offering seats)
- Understand and accommodate menstrual health needs sensitively
- Practice active listening
- Recognize and address unconscious bias
- Create inclusive team environments
- Ensure fair task allocation

### Success Indicators:
- Observable change in everyday behavior towards women employees
- Reduction in complaints from women employees
- Women employees feel welcomed and valued as team members

## 5. Content Structure
### Learning Flow:
- Start with "why this matters" (business case + legal framework)
- Explicitly address unconscious bias
- Build to practical skill application

### Theory/Practice Ratio:
- **Balanced 50/50** (3-4 hours theory, 3-4 hours practice)

### Practice Methods (All included):
- Real scenario discussions (analyzing past incidents anonymously)
- Role-play exercises (practicing polite communication)
- Case study analysis
- Small group activities

### Specific Situations to Cover (All must be included):
- Offering seats / health accommodations (menstrual needs)
- Giving feedback professionally to women employees
- Handling conflicts respectfully
- Daily greetings and interactions
- Professional communication in shop floor context
- Recognizing and responding to discomfort/needs
- Fair task allocation and team inclusion

## 6. Assessment
- **Types:**
  - **Option A:** Participation-based (attendance, engagement in activities, action plan completion)
  - **Option B:** Scenario-based assessment (written/verbal responses to workplace situations)
  - **Option D:** Pre/Post self-assessment (rate own behaviors before and after training)

- **Completion Criteria:**
  - Attendance verification (currently voluntary, no pass/fail)
  - Active participation in all activities
  - Completion of personal action plan

- **Success Indicators:**
  - Demonstrated commitment to specific behavior changes
  - Full attendance
  - Positive feedback from women subordinates

- **Follow-up & Accountability:**
  - 30-day post-training review with each manager
  - Track complaint reduction metrics
  - Managers submit personal action plan at end of training
  - Collect feedback from women employees after 30 days

## 7. Constraints & Logistics
- **Equipment:**
  - U-shape seating arrangement
  - Projector/screen for presentations and videos
- **Materials:**
  - No budget constraints
  - Include videos/external content
  - Printed workbook required
- **Language:** English
- **Cultural Context:** Localize examples to Indian workplace context
- **Facilitator Profile:**
  - External female consultant
  - Behavioral scientist with PhD in Emotional Intelligence
  - Brings credibility on behavior change science and gender perspective
- **Safety:** Sensitive topic - requires psychologically safe learning environment
