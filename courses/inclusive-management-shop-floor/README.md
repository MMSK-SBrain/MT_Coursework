# Inclusive Management for Shop Floor Supervisors

## Course Overview

**Course Title:** Inclusive Management: Building Respectful Shop Floor Leadership
**Course Code:** IMSF
**Duration:** 1 day (9:00 AM - 5:00 PM)
**Target Audience:** Male managers/supervisors (2-15 years experience) in shop floor environments
**Facilitator:** External female consultant, Behavioral scientist with PhD in Emotional Intelligence

## Purpose

This training addresses reported workplace issues around rude behavior, lack of courtesy, and insensitivity toward women employees in shop floor settings. The course focuses on building empathy, emotional intelligence, and inclusive management practices through a balanced mix of theory and hands-on practice.

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, managers will be able to:

1. **Demonstrate respectful and inclusive management practices** toward women employees in shop floor environments
2. **Apply empathy and emotional intelligence** in daily workplace interactions and conflict situations
3. **Implement appropriate workplace accommodations** and support systems for diverse employee needs

## Course Structure

The 1-day training consists of 7 modules:

| Time | Module | Focus |
|------|--------|-------|
| 9:00-10:00 | Foundation - Understanding the Context | Business case, legal framework, impact |
| 10:15-11:15 | Building Self-Awareness | Unconscious bias, emotional intelligence |
| 11:15-12:15 | Developing Empathy | Neuroscience of empathy, perspective-taking |
| 1:00-2:30 | Practical Communication | Daily interactions, feedback, conflict |
| 2:30-3:30 | Health Sensitivity & Courtesies | Menstrual health, accommodations |
| 3:45-4:45 | Application & Integration | Case studies, scenario practice |
| 4:45-5:00 | Commitment & Action Planning | Personal commitments, accountability |

## Delivery Approach

- **Format:** 50% Theory / 50% Practice
- **Methods:** Lectures, videos, self-assessments, role-plays, case studies, group discussions, scenario analysis
- **Language:** English
- **Setup:** U-shape seating, projector/screen, printed workbooks
- **Context:** All examples localized to Indian workplace context

## Assessment

Participants will be assessed through:

1. **Pre/Post Self-Assessment** - Measuring baseline and progress
2. **Scenario-Based Responses** - Applying learned concepts to real situations
3. **Active Participation** - Engagement in all activities
4. **Personal Action Plan** - Commitment to specific behavior changes

**Completion Criteria:**
- Full attendance
- Participation in all activities
- Submission of personal action plan

## Follow-Up

- **30-Day Review:** Individual check-in with each manager
- **Complaint Tracking:** Monitor reduction in reported issues
- **Feedback Collection:** Gather input from women employees on observed changes
