#### Slide 11: Technical Assessment & Control - Earned Value Management
**Visual:** EVM charts showing planned vs actual performance
**Instructor Script:**
> "How do you KNOW if your project is on track? Earned Value Management (EVM).
>
> **EVM Basics:**
>
> **Three key values:**
> - **PV (Planned Value):** Budgeted cost for scheduled work
> - **EV (Earned Value):** Budgeted cost for completed work
> - **AC (Actual Cost):** Actual cost incurred for completed work
>
> **Two key metrics:**
> - **SV (Schedule Variance) = EV - PV**
>   - Positive: Ahead of schedule
>   - Negative: Behind schedule
> - **CV (Cost Variance) = EV - AC**
>   - Positive: Under budget
>   - Negative: Over budget
>
> **Two key indices:**
> - **SPI (Schedule Performance Index) = EV / PV**
>   - > 1.0: Ahead
>   - < 1.0: Behind
> - **CPI (Cost Performance Index) = EV / AC**
>   - > 1.0: Under budget
>   - < 1.0: Over budget
>
> **ADAS Example at Month 12:**
>
> **Planned:** Should have completed 40% of work (PV = $4M)
> **Earned:** Actually completed 30% of work (EV = $3M)
> **Actual Cost:** Spent $3.5M (AC = $3.5M)
>
> **Analysis:**
> - SV = $3M - $4M = -$1M (behind schedule)
> - CV = $3M - $3.5M = -$0.5M (over budget)
> - SPI = 3/4 = 0.75 (25% behind schedule)
> - CPI = 3/3.5 = 0.86 (14% over budget)
>
> **Forecast:**
> - EAC (Estimate at Completion) = BAC / CPI = $10M / 0.86 = $11.6M
> - Expected overrun: $1.6M
>
> **With EVM, ADAP problems would have been visible at Month 12, not Month 24.**
>
> **Automotive standard:** EVM required for programs >$10M (ISO 21500)"

**PPT Content:**
```
TECHNICAL CONTROL: EARNED VALUE MANAGEMENT (EVM)

Key Values:
  • PV (Planned Value): Budget for scheduled work
  • EV (Earned Value): Budget for completed work
  • AC (Actual Cost): Actual cost incurred

Metrics:
  • SV = EV - PV (schedule variance)
  • CV = EV - AC (cost variance)
  • SPI = EV/PV (schedule index)
  • CPI = EV/AC (cost index)

ADAS Example (Month 12):
  PV = $4M | EV = $3M | AC = $3.5M
  → SPI = 0.75 (25% behind)
  → CPI = 0.86 (14% over budget)
  → Forecast: $1.6M overrun

**EVM makes problems visible early**

Tools: MS Project, Primavera, custom dashboards
```

#### Slide 12: Decision Management - Decision Gates
**Visual:** Stage-gate process for automotive development
**Instructor Script:**
> "Decision Management is critical. Let's formalize it.
>
> **ISO/IEC 15288 Decision Management Process:**
> - Prepare for decisions
> - Analyze decision information
> - Make decisions
> - Manage decisions
>
> **Automotive Stage-Gate Process:**
>
> **Gate 0: Concept**
> - Decision: Pursue this system concept?
> - Inputs: Market analysis, feasibility study, business case
> - Criteria: Strategic fit, ROI > 15%, technical feasibility
> - Outputs: Go/No-Go, funding for requirements phase
>
> **Gate 1: Requirements Review**
> - Decision: Requirements complete and stable?
> - Inputs: Requirements document, stakeholder signoff, CONOPS
> - Criteria: 100% traceability, < 5% TBD items, customer approval
> - Outputs: Freeze requirements baseline, proceed to architecture
>
> **Gate 2: Architecture Review**
> - Decision: Architecture viable and approved?
> - Inputs: Architecture description, interface specs, trade studies
> - Criteria: Meets all requirements, passes safety analysis, cost within target
> - Outputs: Freeze architecture baseline, proceed to detailed design
>
> **Gate 3: Design Review (Critical Design Review - CDR)**
> - Decision: Design ready for implementation?
> - Inputs: Detailed designs, simulations, prototype results
> - Criteria: All interfaces defined, V&V plan approved, manufacturing feasible
> - Outputs: Freeze design baseline, proceed to implementation
>
> **Gate 4: System Test Readiness Review**
> - Decision: System ready for full integration?
> - Inputs: Subsystem test results, integration test plan
> - Criteria: All subsystems verified, integration environment ready
> - Outputs: Proceed to system integration
>
> **Gate 5: Production Readiness Review**
> - Decision: Release to production?
> - Inputs: V&V results, certification evidence, field trial data
> - Criteria: All requirements verified, safety certification, customer acceptance
> - Outputs: Release to manufacturing
>
> **Key principles:**
> - Gates are GO/NO-GO decisions, not reviews
> - Criteria defined in advance, objective
> - Cross-functional decision boards
> - Decisions documented and tracked
>
> **ADAP failure:** Architecture decisions made without gate criteria, reversed multiple times."

#### Slide 13: Decision Management - Decision Frameworks
**Visual:** Decision matrix and weighted scoring
**Instructor Script:**
> "Beyond gates, we need frameworks for technical decisions. Two essential tools:
>
> **TOOL 1: Decision Matrix (Pugh Matrix)**
>
> **Use case:** Selecting sensor architecture for ADAS
>
> **Alternatives:**
> - A: Radar + Camera
> - B: Radar + Camera + Lidar
> - C: Camera-only (Tesla approach)
> - D: Radar + Ultrasonic
>
> **Criteria (weighted):**
> - Cost (20%): Capital + lifecycle
> - Range (25%): Detection distance
> - Weather robustness (20%): Rain, fog, snow
> - Accuracy (15%): Position ±cm
> - Redundancy (10%): Fault tolerance
> - Complexity (10%): Integration effort
>
> **Scoring (1-5 scale):**
>
> | Criterion | Weight | A | B | C | D |
> |-----------|--------|---|---|---|---|
> | Cost | 20% | 4 | 2 | 5 | 4 |
> | Range | 25% | 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 |
> | Weather | 20% | 4 | 5 | 2 | 4 |
> | Accuracy | 15% | 3 | 5 | 3 | 2 |
> | Redundancy | 10% | 3 | 5 | 1 | 2 |
> | Complexity | 10% | 4 | 2 | 5 | 4 |
> | **Total** | 100% | 3.75 | 4.15 | 3.25 | 3.25 |
>
> **Result:** Option B (Radar+Camera+Lidar) wins with 4.15 weighted score
>
> **TOOL 2: Real Options Analysis**
>
> **Use case:** When to freeze architecture?
>
> **Options:**
> - **Option 1:** Freeze now (Month 6) → Minimize rework
> - **Option 2:** Defer to Month 9 → Wait for sensor tech maturity
>
> **Analysis:**
> - Value of waiting: New sensor available Month 8, 30% better performance
> - Cost of waiting: 3-month schedule slip, $500K holding cost
> - Probability new sensor works: 70%
>
> **Expected value:**
> - Freeze now: $10M system value
> - Wait: 0.7 × $13M + 0.3 × $10M - $0.5M = $11.6M
>
> **Decision:** Wait for new sensor (higher expected value)
>
> **Key:** Both frameworks require:
> - Defined criteria
> - Quantitative scoring
> - Stakeholder agreement
> - Documentation
>
> This prevents the ADAP ad-hoc decision problem."

```

TOOL 1: Decision Matrix (Pugh Matrix)

Example: ADAS Sensor Selection

  Criteria: Cost, range, weather, accuracy
```

> ✓ Define 5-10 key TPMs (progress, performance, quality)
> ✓ Automate data collection where possible
> ✓ Trend analysis, not just current values
> ✓ Leading indicators to predict problems
> ✓ Weekly TPM review for critical measures
>
> **Configuration & Information:**
> ✓ Establish baselines at gates (functional, allocated, design, product)
> ✓ Single source of truth for each artifact
> ✓ Tool integration to prevent disconnection
> ✓ Version control for all work products

>

## 📝 INSTRUCTOR NOTES

### Pedagogical Strategy

**Approach: Process-Focused/Management**
This session shifts from technical to managerial perspective. The pedagogy emphasizes:

1. **Process Integration**: Show how management processes enable technical processes
2. **Real Consequences**: Use ADAP case to show management process failures
3. **Practical Tools**: EVM, WBS, decision frameworks, TPMs
4. **Organizational Context**: Enabling processes operate at org level
5. **Complete Framework**: All 21 ISO/IEC 15288 processes integrated

**Challenge**: Students often find management processes "boring" compared to technical. Counter by showing ADAP case - technical excellence failed without management processes.

### Common Student Challenges

**Challenge 1**: "Why do we need all this process overhead? Can't we just engineer?"
**Response**: Use ADAP case. Same engineers, same technology. Only difference: processes. Result: Success vs failure. Processes are not overhead, they're enablers.

**Challenge 2**: "EVM seems complicated. Can't we just track % complete?"
**Response**: Show EVM forecasting power. At Month 12, EVM predicted $1.6M overrun. % complete showed "on track." Which would you trust?

**Challenge 3**: "Knowledge management sounds nice but impractical."
**Response**: Calculate cost of repeated mistakes. ADAP repeated integration error: $800K. Capturing and applying one lesson would have saved that. ROI clear.

**Challenge 4**: "These enabling processes are not my job, they're management's job."
**Response**: True, but you'll be affected. Understanding portfolio management helps you understand why your project lost resources. Understanding infrastructure helps you advocate for better tools.
