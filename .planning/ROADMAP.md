# Roadmap: IMSF Assessment Suite

## Overview

This roadmap covers milestone v1.1 — the creation of 8 printable assessment documents for the Inclusive Management for Shop Floor Supervisors (IMSF) training program. Work is organized into three sequential phases, each delivering one complete assessment instrument as a paired set: a participant-facing form and a facilitator-facing guide. Each phase is independently verifiable against physical documents a facilitator can hold, administer, and use on training day.

## Milestone

**v1.1: IMSF Assessment Suite** — 8 documents across 3 assessment instruments

## Phases

- [ ] **Phase 1: Instrument A — Pre/Post Self-Assessment** — Participant Likert form (21 items) and facilitator administration guide; the reflection tool completed twice on training day
- [ ] **Phase 2: Instrument B — Scenario-Based Assessment** — Participant scenario booklet (5 MCQ scenarios) and facilitator answer key with debrief guide; used in Module 6
- [ ] **Phase 3: Instrument C — Personal Action Plan** — Participant action plan template and facilitator evaluation rubric; used in Module 7

## Phase Details

### Phase 1: Instrument A — Pre/Post Self-Assessment

**Goal:** Facilitators have a psychometrically sound, culturally calibrated self-assessment instrument that participants can complete privately in 5 minutes at 9 AM and again at 4:45 PM, and a guide that enables flawless administration of both administrations.

**Depends on:** Nothing (first phase)

**Requirements:** FORM-01, FORM-02, FORM-03, FORM-04, FORM-05, FORM-06, FORM-07, FORM-08, FORM-09, FORM-10, FORM-11, FORM-12, FORM-13, FORM-14, FORM-15, GUIDE-SA-01, GUIDE-SA-02, GUIDE-SA-03, GUIDE-SA-04, GUIDE-SA-05, GUIDE-SA-06, GUIDE-SA-07, GUIDE-SA-08, PROD-01, PROD-02, PROD-03, PROD-04, PROD-05, PROD-06

**Requirement count:** 29 (FORM: 15, GUIDE-SA: 8, PROD: 6)

**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE when this phase is complete):

1. A facilitator can read verbatim from the Pre-assessment script, hand out the form, and complete the administration in 5 minutes with no improvisation required — the script covers the privacy guarantee, behavioral-rating instruction, and silence protocol.
2. The participant form prints correctly on A4 in black and white: 21 items are readable at 11pt minimum, the Pre (AM) and Post (PM) columns are visually distinct (Post column shaded), all 7 domain groups are separated by visual breaks, and there are no section labels visible to the participant.
3. Every item on the form reads as a first-person, present-tense, behavioral statement describing a specific observable action (not an attitude or intention), with Health Sensitivity domain items using health-general language that does not name menstruation.
4. The facilitator guide correctly identifies all 3 reverse-scored items by number and provides the full conversion table (1->5, 2->4, 3->3, 4->2, 5->1); the participant form carries no indication of which items are reverse-scored.
5. A facilitator using only the guide can collect aggregate room-level data via the specified show-of-hands protocol without collecting any participant forms — the guide states explicitly that all forms go home with participants.

**Plans:** 3 plans

Plans:
- [ ] 01-01-PLAN.md — Generate participant self-assessment form (.docx) with all 21 items, Pre/Post columns, self-reflection section, and domain-averaging grid (FORM-01..15, PROD-01..03)
- [ ] 01-02-PLAN.md — Generate facilitator administration guide (.docx) with domain map, reverse-scoring, verbatim scripts, show-of-hands protocol, and cultural notes (GUIDE-SA-01..08, PROD-04)

### Phase 2: Instrument B — Scenario-Based Assessment

**Goal:** Facilitators have a 5-scenario MCQ booklet with authentic Indian shop floor vignettes and a companion debrief guide that enables a structured 60-minute Module 6 session (15 min individual + 40 min debrief + 5 min scoring) without requiring the facilitator to improvise content, manage defensive reactions, or decide how much time to spend per scenario.

**Depends on:** Phase 1 (production standards and file conventions established)

**Requirements:** SCEN-01, SCEN-02, SCEN-03, SCEN-04, SCEN-05, SCEN-06, SCEN-07, SCEN-08, SCEN-09, SCEN-10, SCEN-11, SCEN-12, SCEN-13, GUIDE-SC-01, GUIDE-SC-02, GUIDE-SC-03, GUIDE-SC-04, GUIDE-SC-05, GUIDE-SC-06, GUIDE-SC-07, GUIDE-SC-08, GUIDE-SC-09, GUIDE-SC-10, GUIDE-SC-11, GUIDE-SC-12

**Requirement count:** 25 (SCEN: 13, GUIDE-SC: 12)

**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE when this phase is complete):

1. A facilitator can open the debrief guide to any of the 5 scenarios and immediately read: the best answer letter, the point value for each option (3/2/1/0), a 3-4 sentence rationale connecting back to specific course concepts, one key teaching point per non-best option, and the time allocation — with no additional preparation required.
2. The participant booklet prints as a self-contained document: instructions page, 5 vignettes (60-90 words each) with 4 options (A-D) starting with action verbs, a detachable answer sheet with scoring bands (13-15, 9-12, 5-8, 0-4), and the explicit statement that scores are not shared with HR — all without any option being obviously cartoonishly bad or telegraphing the best answer through length or vocabulary.
3. Best answer positions are distributed across the 5 scenarios (no more than 2 consecutive in the same position; at least 3 of 4 positions A/B/C/D used), and scenario topics follow the prescribed difficulty arc: Easy (greeting) -> Easy-Medium (health accommodation) -> Medium (feedback) -> Medium-Hard (peer rudeness) -> Medium (task allocation).
4. The facilitator guide specifies 2-3 discussion questions per scenario categorized by type (personal reflection / real workplace connection / challenge of the Adequate answer), and the health accommodation scenario (Scenario 2) includes an exact facilitator response to the question "But how do we know it's her period?"
5. The facilitator guide includes the four named facilitation techniques — Show of Hands, Devil's Advocate Reframe, Real Story Bridge, No Shame Frame — each with example language the facilitator can deliver verbatim during the debrief.

### Phase 3: Instrument C — Personal Action Plan

**Goal:** Facilitators have a structured action plan template that architecturally forces behavioral specificity (using the When/Will/Instead-of formula) and a facilitator rubric with coaching tools that enable real-time intervention during Module 7 circulation — so that by the end of the Commitment Circle, every participant has at least two actionable, observable commitments written in their own hand.

**Depends on:** Phase 2 (Module 6 precedes Module 7; debrief feeds into action planning)

**Requirements:** PLAN-01, PLAN-02, PLAN-03, PLAN-04, PLAN-05, PLAN-06, PLAN-07, PLAN-08, PLAN-09, PLAN-10, PLAN-11, PLAN-12, PLAN-13, PLAN-14, PLAN-15, GUIDE-AP-01, GUIDE-AP-02, GUIDE-AP-03, GUIDE-AP-04, GUIDE-AP-05, GUIDE-AP-06, GUIDE-AP-07, GUIDE-AP-08, GUIDE-AP-09, GUIDE-AP-10

**Requirement count:** 25 (PLAN: 15, GUIDE-AP: 10)

**Success Criteria** (what must be TRUE when this phase is complete):

1. The participant form prints correctly on A4 double-sided at 120gsm or heavier: all 7 sections are present in sequence (Header, Reflection Anchor, three Commitment Slots, Coverage Check, Commitment Circle Prep, Peer Table, Two-Week Self-Check), the commitment formula "When [situation], I will [action] instead of [old habit]" is printed at the top of the commitment section with one model example, and the form's visual design reads as a professional leadership development document (no clip art, adequate line height for handwriting).
2. A facilitator circulating during Module 7 can use the rubric to evaluate any participant's commitment on two dimensions (Specificity and Depth), each with 3 named levels (Strong/Developing/Vague), and select the correct facilitator action from the 5-cell Coaching Decision Matrix without stopping to interpret the rubric.
3. The escalating coaching question sequence (4 questions from "Tell me about this one" through "Can you rewrite this as: When [situation], I will [action]?") is printed in the guide in order, with tone guidance specifying crouching posture, "this is a good start" opening language, and the explicit prohibition on saying "too vague."
4. The facilitator guide includes a minute-by-minute Module 7 timing script from 4:45 to 5:00 PM and the full Commitment Circle script (opening statement, round format, "Starting tomorrow, I will..." instruction, and closing statement) — so the facilitator can run the final 15 minutes verbatim without notes.
5. The perforated wallet-card tear-off, the phone calendar reminder prompt ("Put this date in your phone calendar now: ____"), the commitment signature line, and the "not submitted to HR" statement all appear on the participant form — each present and legible at the correct print size.
