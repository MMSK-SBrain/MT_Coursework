**Part 2: Engine Oil and Filter Service (20 min)**

Priya raised the vehicle on the lift and positioned drain pans underneath.

**Oil Drain Procedure:**

1. **Warm engine briefly:** Started engine for 2 minutes to warm oil (flows easier when warm)
2. **Position drain pan:** Large 10-liter capacity pan under drain plug
3. **Remove drain plug:** 17mm wrench, careful not to drop plug into hot oil
4. **Drain completely:** Waited 5 minutes for all oil to drain (Maruti 1.5L K-series engine capacity: 3.7 liters)
5. **Inspect drain plug:** Checked for metal particles on magnetic drain plug
   - **Finding:** Small amount of normal fine particles (expected at 40k km)
   - **Action:** Cleaned plug with solvent

**Oil Filter Replacement:**

1. **Locate filter:** Cartridge-type filter on right side of engine block
2. **Remove filter:** Oil filter wrench, careful of hot oil dripping
3. **Inspect old filter:** Cut open to check for metal particles (quality check)
   - **Finding:** Clean filter media, no abnormal metal content
4. **Prepare new filter:**
   - Applied clean engine oil to O-ring seal
   - Pre-filled filter with new oil (prevents dry start)
5. **Install new filter:** Hand-tight plus 3/4 turn (per Maruti specification)

**New Oil Fill:**

1. **Verified correct oil:** Maruti specified 5W-30 synthetic (BS6 compliant)
2. **Fill quantity:** 3.5 liters (check dipstick after running)
3. **Install drain plug:** New copper crush washer, torque to 35 Nm
4. **Start engine:** Let run for 30 seconds, check for leaks
5. **Check level:** Waited 2 minutes, checked dipstick, topped off to full mark

**Priya's Professional Habit:**

She recorded oil service details on the sticker:
- Oil type: 5W-30 Synthetic
- Date: Today's date
- Odometer: 39,847 km
- Next service: 49,847 km (10,000 km interval)

Placed sticker on door jamb.

*Attention to detail. This is what separates professionals from quick-change shops.*

**Part 3: Air Filters and Spark Plugs (25 min)**

**Engine Air Filter:**

1. **Remove air filter housing:** 4 clips, lift cover carefully
2. **Inspect old filter:** Heavily contaminated with dust (Indian road conditions)
   - Priya took a photo to show customer
3. **Clean housing:** Wiped out dust and debris with cloth
4. **Install new filter:** Ensured proper seating, no gaps around edge
5. **Secure housing:** All 4 clips firmly closed

**Cabin Air Filter:**

1. **Locate filter:** Behind glove box (removed glove box for access)
2. **Remove old filter:** Pulled filter out carefully
   - **Finding:** Heavily clogged with leaves, dust, pollen
   - Priya took another photo (great visual for customer)
3. **Clean housing cavity:** Vacuumed out debris
4. **Install new filter:** Arrow pointing toward airflow direction (marked on filter)
5. **Reinstall glove box:** All clips secure

*Customer will notice immediate difference in A/C performance. This is value they can feel.*

**Spark Plug Replacement:**

1. **Remove ignition coils:** 4 coils, 10mm bolt each, disconnect electrical connector
2. **Inspect coil boots:** Check for cracks or carbon tracking
   - All boots in good condition
3. **Remove old plugs:** 16mm spark plug socket with rubber insert
4. **Inspect old plugs:**
   - Gap worn to 1.2mm (spec: 0.8-0.9mm)
   - Electrodes rounded (normal wear at 40k km)
   - Light tan color (correct fuel mixture)
   - Priya saved one plug to show customer
5. **Prepare new plugs:**
   - Verified gap: 0.8mm (checked with gap tool)
   - Applied anti-seize to threads (prevents seizure)
6. **Install new plugs:** Hand-tight, then 1/4 turn with wrench (torque: 20 Nm)
7. **Reinstall coils:** Ensure proper seating, torque bolts to 10 Nm
8. **Start engine:** Smooth idle (plug replacement successful)

**The Learning Moment:**

As Priya inspected the old spark plugs, she remembered her instructor's demonstration: *"Read the plugs like a diagnostic tool. Color tells you fuel mixture. Wear tells you ignition system health. Damage tells you engine problems."*

The tan color and normal wear confirmed: This engine is running well. Good news for the customer.

**Part 4: Brake Fluid Service (25 min)**

This was the service Priya was most careful about—brake systems are safety-critical.

**Brake Fluid Flush Procedure:**

1. **Check current fluid:** Reservoir level good, but fluid dark brown (moisture absorbed over time)
2. **Test fluid condition:** Brake fluid tester showed 3.5% moisture content
   - Specification: Replace if over 3%
   - **Action required:** Flush and replace
3. **Set up pressure bleeder:**
   - Filled pressure bleeder with new DOT 3 brake fluid
   - Pressurized to 20 psi
   - Connected to master cylinder reservoir
4. **Bleed sequence:** Right rear → Left rear → Right front → Left front (farthest to closest)
5. **Bleeding each wheel:**
   - Attached clear hose to bleeder valve
   - Opened valve 1/2 turn
   - Watched fluid flow until color changed from dark brown to light amber (new fluid)
   - Closed valve: Torque to 10 Nm
   - Repeated for all four wheels
6. **Total fluid used:** 750ml (flushed entire system)
7. **Final check:**
   - Reservoir filled to MAX line with new fluid
   - Brake pedal firm and high
   - Started engine, pedal dropped slightly (normal with brake booster)
   - Pumped pedal 10 times, remained firm (no air in system)

**Quality Verification:**

Priya performed the critical safety check:
- Pedal height: 130mm from floor (per Maruti specification)
- Pedal firmness: Solid, no spongy feel
- No leaks at any bleeder valves or connections

*Safety is non-negotiable. If brakes aren't perfect, vehicle doesn't leave the bay.*

## Assessment Content

### Quiz Questions (5 Questions - PowerPoint Slide Format)

**Question 1: Scheduled Maintenance Intervals (Remember Level)**

According to Maruti service schedule, which of the following is typically included in a 40,000 km service?

A) Oil change only
B) Oil change, air filter, spark plugs, brake fluid flush
C) Timing belt replacement
D) Transmission overhaul

**Answer:** B) Oil change, air filter, spark plugs, brake fluid flush
**Explanation:** 40,000 km is a major service interval that includes oil/filter, air filter, cabin filter, spark plugs, and brake fluid flush. Timing belt is typically 80,000-100,000 km. Transmission service intervals vary by type.

---

**Question 2: Multi-Point Inspection Priority (Evaluate Level)**

During multi-point inspection, you find a drive belt with small cracks and a CV boot with a small crack. Which priority classification is correct?

A) Both are "immediate" (unsafe to drive)
B) Drive belt: "soon"; CV boot: "immediate"
C) Both are "soon" (need attention within 1-2 months)
D) Both are "optional" (monitor only)

**Answer:** C) Both are "soon" (need attention within 1-2 months)
**Explanation:** Cracked drive belt will fail eventually but isn't immediate danger. Cracked CV boot will lead to CV joint failure within 1-2 months if not repaired. Both are "soon" priority—safe to drive now but need repair before next long trip.

---

**Question 3: Brake Fluid Service (Apply Level)**

When performing brake fluid flush, what is the correct bleeding sequence for a standard vehicle?

A) Left front → Right front → Left rear → Right rear
B) Right rear → Left rear → Right front → Left front
C) Any sequence is acceptable
D) Front wheels only

**Answer:** B) Right rear → Left rear → Right front → Left front
**Explanation:** Bleed from farthest wheel from master cylinder to closest. This ensures all old fluid is completely flushed and no air is trapped. Standard sequence: Right rear → Left rear → Right front → Left front.

---

**Question 4: Customer Communication (Apply Level)**

A customer is concerned about repair costs. What is the BEST way to communicate a CV boot replacement recommendation?

A) "Your CV boot is broken. You need to fix it. It costs ₹2,500."
B) "I recommend replacing the CV boot. It has a crack with minor grease leakage. If we replace the boot now for ₹2,500, it prevents CV joint failure which costs ₹12,000-₹15,000 to repair later."
C) "Your CV boot is cracked. If you don't fix it, your car will break down on the highway and you'll be stranded with your family."
D) "The CV boot has a crack. It's up to you whether you want to fix it or not."

**Answer:** B) "I recommend replacing the CV boot. It has a crack with minor grease leakage. If we replace the boot now for ₹2,500, it prevents CV joint failure which costs ₹12,000-₹15,000 to repair later."
**Explanation:** Professional communication explains WHAT is wrong, WHY it matters, and WHAT happens if not addressed, with cost comparison. No scare tactics (option C), no vague statements (option D), and not just directive without explanation (option A).

---

**Question 5: Inspection Documentation (Apply Level)**

During multi-point inspection, what is the BEST way to document findings for customer presentation?

A) Write text description only
B) Take photos of issues found and include in inspection report
C) Verbally explain findings without documentation
D) Only document if customer asks for proof

**Answer:** B) Take photos of issues found and include in inspection report
**Explanation:** Visual evidence (photos) combined with written documentation is most effective for customer understanding and authorization. Customers trust what they can see. Professional practice includes photos of wear items, leaks, and conditions requiring attention.

### Hands-On Exercise Preview (Afternoon Practical - 3 Hours)

**Exercise Title:** Complete Preventive Maintenance Service with Customer Presentation

**Objective:**
Execute manufacturer scheduled maintenance service (20,000 km or 40,000 km), perform comprehensive multi-point inspection, document findings with photos, and present recommendations to "customer" (instructor) professionally.

**Step-by-Step Activity:**

**Setup (15 min):**
1. Students work in pairs (one technician, one customer/quality checker, then swap)
2. Each pair assigned one vehicle:
   - Maruti Swift (20,000 km service)
   - Hyundai i20 (40,000 km service)
   - Tata Nexon (20,000 km service)
   - Honda City (40,000 km service)
3. Service schedule provided for each vehicle (manufacturer specifications)
4. Multi-point inspection checklist (25 items)
5. Tablet or phone for documenting findings with photos
6. Customer presentation template

**Phase 1: Scheduled Service Execution (90 min):**

**Services to Complete (varies by vehicle and interval):**
- Engine oil and filter change
- Air filter replacement (if due)
- Cabin filter replacement (if due)
- Spark plug replacement (if 40k interval)
- Brake fluid service or check (if due)
- Coolant check and top-up
- Transmission fluid level check
- All fluid levels verified
- Service interval reset

**Quality Standards:**
- Torque specifications followed (drain plug, filter, wheels)
- Correct fluid types used (per manufacturer spec)
- No leaks after service
- Service reminder stickers installed
- Time management: Complete scheduled service within 90 minutes

**Phase 2: Multi-Point Inspection (45 min):**

**25-Point Inspection Checklist:**

**Underhood (8 items):**
1. Battery voltage and condition
2. Drive belt condition and tension
3. Engine mounts
4. Coolant hoses
5. Vacuum lines
6. Wiring and connectors
7. Fluid leaks
8. Exhaust system

**Brake System (5 items):**
9. Front brake pad thickness
10. Rear brake pad/shoe thickness
11. Brake rotor/drum condition
12. Brake lines and hoses
13. Parking brake operation

**Suspension & Steering (6 items):**
14. Shock absorbers
15. Ball joints and tie rod ends
16. Bushings (control arm, sway bar)
17. Steering play
18. CV boots (FWD vehicles)
19. Wheel bearings

**Tires (3 items):**
20. Tread depth (all 4 tires)
21. Tire pressure (adjust to spec)
22. Tire condition (cuts, bulges, uneven wear)

**Lights & Electrical (3 items):**
23. All exterior lights operation
24. Wiper blade condition
25. Battery and charging system

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Check each item on inspection sheet
- Mark status: OK / Attention Needed / Immediate
- Take photos of ANY item needing attention
- Measure critical items (tread depth, brake pad thickness, fluid levels)
- Note odometer reading and date

**Phase 3: Customer Presentation (30 min total, 15 min per student):**

**Presentation Structure:**

1. **Introduction (1 min):**
   - Greet "customer" (instructor) professionally
   - Overview: "Your vehicle is ready. Let me walk you through what I completed and what I found."

2. **Services Completed (3 min):**
   - List all scheduled services performed
   - Show visual evidence (new air filter vs. old, new spark plugs vs. old)
   - Confirm cost matches original estimate

3. **Inspection Findings (8 min):**
   - Present findings organized by priority:
     - **Immediate:** Unsafe to drive, must repair now
     - **Soon:** Safe to drive but needs attention within 1-2 months
     - **Recommended:** Not urgent but improves safety/reliability
     - **Optional:** Nice to have, not necessary
   - Show photo evidence for each finding
   - Explain WHY each item needs attention (not just WHAT)
   - Provide cost estimate for each recommendation

4. **Questions and Authorization (3 min):**
   - Answer customer questions
   - Obtain authorization for additional work (or note declined recommendations)
   - Summarize total cost

**Instructor Evaluation Criteria:**

**Technical Execution (50 points):**
- Scheduled service completed correctly: 20 points
- Multi-point inspection thorough: 20 points
- Documentation complete with photos: 10 points

**Customer Communication (30 points):**
- Professional presentation manner: 10 points
- Clear, non-technical explanations: 10 points
- Visual evidence shown: 5 points
- Priority classifications correct: 5 points

**Time Management (10 points):**
- Completed within 3-hour time limit: 10 points

**Safety (10 points):**
- 100% safety compliance throughout: 10 points
- Proper torque procedures: Pass/Fail (Fail = zero points)

**Total: 100 points (70% pass mark = 70 points)**

**Common Challenges (Instructor Will Address):**
1. **Rushing inspection:** Taking shortcuts, missing wear items
2. **Poor documentation:** Not taking photos, incomplete notes
3. **Weak customer communication:** Technical jargon, no visual evidence, unclear priorities
4. **Time management:** Taking too long, not planning workflow efficiently

**Safety Reminders:**
- Vehicle must be safely supported before working underneath
- Torque specifications non-negotiable (especially wheel lug nuts)
- Dispose of waste oil, filters, and fluids properly
- Brake fluid is corrosive—avoid skin contact and paint damage
- Always clean up work area after service
