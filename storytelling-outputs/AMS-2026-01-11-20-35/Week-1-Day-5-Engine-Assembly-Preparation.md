**Step 2: Block Preparation and Main Bearing Installation (9:30 AM - 10:00 AM)**

**Cleanliness Check:**

Priya wiped the block deck surface with a clean white cloth. The cloth came away spotless. "Clean means no contamination. Contamination means bearing failure."

**Main Bearing Shell Installation:**

Maruti WagonR 1.0L has a **4-bearing crankshaft** (4-cylinder, compact design).

**Installation Sequence:**

1. **Oil passages cleaned:** Priya blew compressed air through all oil galleries in block. Air flowed freely ✓

2. **Upper bearing shells installed:** Each shell has a locating tab that fits into a notch in the block. She installed all four upper shells, ensuring tabs engaged properly.

3. **Pre-lubrication:** Using assembly lube (high-viscosity lubricant that won't drip off), she coated each bearing surface. "First start is critical. Assembly lube protects until oil pump begins circulating."

---

**Step 3: Crankshaft Installation (10:00 AM - 10:30 AM)**

Priya lifted the crankshaft (approximately 12 kg for WagonR 1.0L) and carefully lowered it into position. The crank journals sat perfectly on the upper bearing shells.

**Bearing Clearance Verification (Plastigage Method):**

Kumar sir required Plastigage verification on bearing #1 before proceeding.

**Procedure:**
1. Placed Plastigage strand across crankshaft journal #1
2. Installed lower bearing shell in main cap
3. Installed main cap, finger-tightened bolts
4. Torqued bolts to specification: 35 Nm + 45° angle
5. Removed cap carefully (did NOT rotate crankshaft)
6. Measured flattened Plastigage width: 0.04mm ✓

"Perfect clearance. Proceeding with all bearing caps."

---

**Main Bearing Cap Installation - The Critical Torque Sequence:**

Service manual torque sequence for WagonR 1.0L 4-bearing crankshaft:
- **Sequence:** 2 (center-front) → 3 (center-rear) → 1 (front) → 4 (rear)
- **Why center-first?** Centers crankshaft in bore, prevents distortion

**Torque Specification:** 35 Nm + 45° angle (torque-to-yield bolts)

**Priya's Procedure:**

1. **Lubricated bearing shells:** Assembly lube on all lower shells
2. **Installed caps:** Verified arrow marks face front (orientation critical)
3. **Finger-tightened bolts:** All eight bolts (two per cap)
4. **Torqued in sequence:**
   - Main cap #2: 35 Nm, then marked bolt heads with paint pen, then rotated 45° past mark
   - Main cap #3: Same procedure
   - Main cap #1: Same procedure
   - Main cap #4: Same procedure

5. **Verification:** Rotated crankshaft by hand. Smooth rotation, no binding ✓

---

**Crankshaft End Play Check:**

Priya set up a dial indicator against crankshaft flange, pried crankshaft forward and backward with pry bar.

**Total movement:** 0.08mm (specification: 0.05-0.15mm) ✓

"End play within spec. Crankshaft installation complete."

Kumar sir inspected her work. "Perfect torque marks, correct sequence, smooth rotation. Well done. Continue."

### Climax: Phase 2 - Bottom End Assembly (50 minutes)
**"The piston rings must seal perfectly. Orientation and gap position are everything."**

**Step 4: Piston Ring Installation (10:45 AM - 11:30 AM)**

**The Three Rings Per Piston:**
1. **Top compression ring:** Chrome-faced, marked "TOP" (must face up)
2. **Second compression ring:** Tapered face (taper face down)
3. **Oil control ring:** Three-piece (two rails + expander)

**Piston #1 Ring Installation:**

**Oil Control Ring (Bottom, Installed First):**
- Installed expander (spring), ensured ends butted (not overlapped)
- Installed lower rail, positioned gap 180° from expander gap
- Installed upper rail, positioned gap 180° from lower rail gap

**Second Compression Ring:**
- Verified taper face down (felt edge with fingernail—one edge sharp = taper face)
- Used ring expander tool (prevents over-expanding and breaking ring)
- Positioned gap 90° from oil ring gaps

**Top Compression Ring:**
- Verified "TOP" mark faces up
- Installed with ring expander tool
- Positioned gap 180° from second ring gap

**Final Ring Gap Positions (Clock Positions):**
- Top ring: 12 o'clock
- Second ring: 6 o'clock
- Oil rail upper: 3 o'clock
- Oil expander: 9 o'clock
- Oil rail lower: 12 o'clock (opposite side from top ring)

**Why Stagger Gaps?**

"If all gaps align, combustion gases blow straight through. Staggered gaps create labyrinth seal."

**All Four Pistons:**

Priya installed rings on all four pistons, photographing each piston's gap positions for her documentation.

Time invested: 45 minutes for four pistons. "Rushed ring installation = broken rings. Take the time."

**Step 5: Piston/Rod Assembly Installation (11:30 AM - 12:15 PM)**

**The Challenge:**

Pistons must enter cylinders without damaging rings (rings extend beyond piston diameter and will catch on cylinder edge).

**The Tool: Ring Compressor**

**Procedure for Piston #1:**

1. **Pre-lubrication:** Coated piston, rings, and cylinder wall with assembly lube

2. **Orientation verification:**
   - Connecting rod marked "1" (cylinder number) faces exhaust side
   - Arrow on piston crown faces front of engine ✓

3. **Ring compressor installed:** Tightened band around piston, compressing all rings flush with piston

4. **Inserted into cylinder:** Placed piston/rod assembly at top of cylinder #1, tapped gently with wooden hammer handle

5. **Careful pushing:** Piston slid into cylinder. At halfway point, ring compressor fell away. Continued pushing until piston seated at bottom

6. **Rod bearing installation:**
   - Installed rod bearing shells (upper and lower)
   - Pre-lubricated with assembly lube
   - Positioned rod over crankshaft rod journal #1
   - Installed rod cap (verified rod numbers match: 1 to 1)
   - Torqued: 25 Nm + 60° angle

7. **Verification:** Rotated crankshaft. Piston moved smoothly up and down ✓

**Pistons #2, #3, #4:**

Priya repeated the process three more times. Each piston took approximately 10 minutes.

**Final Check:**

All four pistons installed. She rotated the crankshaft two full revolutions. Smooth, even resistance throughout. No binding.

**New Torque-to-Yield Bolts:**

**Torque Sequence (WagonR 1.0L):**

Service manual showed numbered pattern:
- Start: Center bolts (5, 6)
- Next: Inner ring (3, 4, 7, 8)
- Last: Outer bolts (1, 2, 9, 10)

**Three-Stage Torque Procedure:**

**Stage 1: Initial Torque (15 Nm)**
- Torqued all 10 bolts in sequence to 15 Nm (finger-tight, ensures even seating)

**Stage 2: Final Torque (40 Nm)**
- Torqued all 10 bolts in sequence to 40 Nm

**Stage 3: Angle Tightening (90° + 90°)**
- Starting with bolt #5, marked bolt head with paint pen, rotated 90° past mark
- Continued through sequence: All bolts 90°
- **Second angle pass:** Torqued all bolts additional 90° (total 180° angle after Stage 2)

**Why Three Stages?**

"Stage 1 seats the gasket evenly. Stage 2 clamps to spec. Stage 3 stretches bolts into yield zone, providing consistent clamping force as gasket compresses over time."

## Technical Deep Dive (Integrated Learning)

### Bearing Installation and Clearance (SO-1-5-2 Details)

**Bearing Shell Installation Rules:**
1. **Clean surfaces:** No oil on shell backs (prevents shell spinning in bore)
2. **Locating tabs engage:** Tab must seat in notch (prevents shell rotation)
3. **No force required:** Shells snap in with hand pressure only
4. **Upper shell installed first:** Crankshaft then placed on upper shells

**Plastigage Clearance Verification:**

**Acceptable Clearance:** 0.025-0.065mm (typical small engines)

**Interpreting Results:**
- Too tight (< 0.025mm): Bearing overheats, may seize
- Correct (0.025-0.065mm): Oil film maintained
- Too loose (> 0.075mm): Low oil pressure, noise, excessive wear

**Crankshaft End Play:**

**Purpose:** Allows thermal expansion while preventing excessive axial movement

**Specification:** 0.05-0.15mm (typical)

**How to Check:**
1. Mount dial indicator against crank flange
2. Pry crankshaft forward (toward indicator)
3. Zero indicator
4. Pry crankshaft backward (away from indicator)
5. Note total movement = end play

---

### Piston Ring Installation (SO-1-5-1 Details)

**Ring Orientation:**

**Top Compression Ring:**
- Marked "TOP" or "T" (must face up)
- Usually chrome or nitrided face (wear resistance)

**Second Compression Ring:**
- Tapered face or marked "TOP"
- Taper face DOWN (scrapes oil downward)

**Oil Control Ring (3-Piece):**
- Expander (spring) installed first
- Lower rail installed second
- Upper rail installed last
- Gaps staggered

**Ring End Gap Positioning:**

**Why Stagger?**
- Aligned gaps = direct path for combustion pressure blow-by
- Staggered gaps create labyrinth seal

**Common Gap Positions:**
- Top ring: 12 o'clock (or 0°)
- Second ring: 180° opposite (6 o'clock)
- Oil rails: 90° from compression rings (3 and 9 o'clock)
- Oil expander: Between rails

---

### Cylinder Head Torque Procedure (SO-1-5-3 Details)

**Torque-to-Yield (TTY) Bolts:**

**What are TTY bolts?**
- Bolts designed to stretch into plastic deformation zone
- Provides consistent clamping force as gasket compresses
- MUST be replaced (cannot be reused)

**Three-Stage Torque Method:**

**Stage 1: Initial Torque (15-20 Nm typical)**
- Purpose: Seat gasket evenly, prevent misalignment
- Light clamping, no stretching yet

**Stage 2: Final Torque (40-60 Nm typical)**
- Purpose: Clamp head to specification
- Bolts still in elastic zone (could theoretically be reused after this stage)

**Stage 3: Angle Tightening (60°-120° typical, sometimes multiple passes)**
- Purpose: Stretch bolt into yield zone
- Bolt elongates permanently
- Provides consistent clamping even as gasket compresses over time

**Torque Sequence (General Rule):**
- Start center, work outward
- Alternating pattern (prevents warping)
- Follow service manual sequence exactly

---

### Pre-Lubrication (SO-1-5-1 Details)

**Why Pre-Lubricate?**

First start is most critical:
- Oil pump needs 3-5 seconds to build pressure
- Bearings and cylinder walls are dry
- Metal-to-metal contact causes immediate wear

**Assembly Lube vs. Engine Oil:**

**Assembly Lube (Preferred):**
- High-viscosity, tacky (won't drip off during assembly)
- Contains anti-wear additives
- Remains on surfaces for weeks if needed

**Engine Oil (Acceptable):**
- Will drip off vertical surfaces
- Provides some protection
- Less ideal than assembly lube

**Critical Pre-Lube Points:**
- All bearing surfaces (main, rod, cam)
- Cylinder walls
- Piston skirts and rings
- Camshaft lobes

## Assessment Content

### Quiz Questions (5 Questions)

**Question 1: Bearing Clearance (Apply Level)**

You measure bearing clearance using Plastigage and get 0.09mm. Specification is 0.025-0.065mm. What action is required?

A) Acceptable, install and continue
B) Too loose; install undersize bearing shells
C) Too tight; file bearing shells to increase clearance
D) Remeasure using different Plastigage

**Answer:** B) Too loose; install undersize bearing shells
**Explanation:** 0.09mm exceeds maximum spec (0.065mm). Excessive clearance causes low oil pressure and noise. Solution is undersize bearing shells (0.25mm undersize reduces clearance by 0.25mm to 0.065mm, within spec). Never file bearing shells—damages surface.

---

**Question 2: Piston Ring Orientation (Apply Level)**

You are installing piston rings. The second compression ring has one sharp edge and one smooth edge. How should it be installed?

A) Sharp edge up
B) Sharp edge down
C) Orientation doesn't matter
D) Install with "TOP" marking up

**Answer:** B) Sharp edge down
**Explanation:** Second compression ring is tapered. Sharp edge = taper face. Taper face must point DOWN (scrapes oil downward during upstroke). Incorrect orientation causes oil consumption. If ring has "TOP" marking, follow marking. If no marking, feel edges.

---

**Question 3: Head Bolt Torque Sequence (Understand Level)**

Why must cylinder head bolts be torqued starting from center and working outward?

A) Saves time by following bolt number order
B) Prevents head warping by distributing clamping force evenly from center
C) Easier access to center bolts first
D) Manufacturer preference with no technical reason

**Answer:** B) Prevents head warping by distributing clamping force evenly from center
**Explanation:** Starting at center and working outward ensures gasket seats evenly without trapping high spots that cause warping. Random sequence creates stress concentration, warping head and causing leaks. Always follow service manual sequence.

---

**Question 4: Plastigage Measurement (Apply Level)**

After torquing a main bearing cap with Plastigage installed, what is the CORRECT next step?

A) Rotate crankshaft to spread Plastigage, then remove cap and measure
B) Remove cap without rotating crankshaft, then measure Plastigage width
C) Leave cap installed and read clearance through oil hole
D) Start engine and check for bearing noise

**Answer:** B) Remove cap without rotating crankshaft, then measure Plastigage width
**Explanation:** Plastigage flattens under torque pressure. Rotating crankshaft smears Plastigage, making measurement inaccurate. Correct procedure: Torque cap, remove cap carefully (no rotation), measure flattened Plastigage width using scale on package. Width correlates to clearance.

---

**Question 5: Ring End Gap Positioning (Understand Level)**

Why must piston ring end gaps be staggered (not aligned)?

A) Easier installation if gaps are separated
B) Aligned gaps create direct path for combustion pressure blow-by
C) Rings last longer if gaps don't touch
D) Prevents rings from tangling during installation

**Answer:** B) Aligned gaps create direct path for combustion pressure blow-by
**Explanation:** Ring gaps allow tiny amount of combustion pressure past rings (inevitable). If all gaps align, pressure blows straight through into crankcase (excessive blow-by). Staggered gaps create labyrinth seal—pressure must travel around piston multiple times, much less escapes.
