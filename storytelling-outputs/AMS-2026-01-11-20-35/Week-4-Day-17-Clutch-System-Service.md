11. Remove right driveshaft (same procedure)
12. Remove engine mount and transmission mount bolts
13. Remove bell housing bolts (four bolts, note different lengths)
14. Slide transmission straight back, lower carefully

**Arjun:** "Ramesh sir, transmission is stuck. Should I pry it?"

**Ramesh:** "Never pry. What did I teach you yesterday?"

Ramesh inspects: "Look here—one bell housing bolt hidden behind starter motor. Remove that, try again."

Arjun removes the hidden bolt. Transmission separates smoothly.

**Lesson Learned:** Always verify all fasteners removed before applying force. Hidden bolts are common on compact cars.

**10:45 AM - Transmission removed successfully**

**Arjun inspects:**

- Transmission input shaft seal: Slight oil seepage visible

**Ramesh:** "We replace that seal during reassembly. If we don't, the new clutch will slip prematurely. Add it to the customer estimate—seal costs ₹150, but saves the new clutch. Always explain to customers why additional parts are needed."

**Part 5: Clutch Installation (20 min)**

**11:15 AM - Installation Begins**

**Step 1: Flywheel Surface Preparation**
- Arjun cleans flywheel with brake cleaner
- Uses Scotch-Brite pad to remove glazing
- Wipes clean with lint-free cloth
- **Critical:** No oil, grease, or fingerprints on friction surface

**Step 2: Replace Transmission Input Shaft Seal**
- Remove old seal with seal puller
- Clean seal bore
- Install new seal using seal driver (correct size—doesn't damage seal lip)
- Apply thin coat of grease to seal lip (prevents dry startup)

**Step 3: Install Clutch Disc and Pressure Plate**

**Ramesh:** "This is the critical step. Clutch disc must be centered perfectly, or transmission won't install. Use the alignment tool."

**Arjun's Installation Sequence:**

1. Place clutch disc on flywheel (friction material facing pressure plate side)
   - **Check orientation:** "Which side faces the flywheel?" Arjun asks.
   - **Ramesh:** "Look at the hub. The side with more offset (depth) faces the pressure plate. Stamped arrow or 'Flywheel Side' marking shows correct orientation."
   - Arjun checks: Marking says "Flywheel Side"—installs correctly.

2. Place pressure plate over clutch disc
   - Align bolt holes with flywheel threaded holes
   - Install bolts finger-tight (do NOT torque yet)

3. Insert clutch alignment tool through disc hub into pilot bearing
   - Tool centers the disc on flywheel centerline
   - **Purpose:** Ensures transmission input shaft can slide through disc hub during installation

4. Torque pressure plate bolts
   - **Sequence:** Star pattern (tighten opposite bolts alternately)
   - **Torque:** 15 Nm (finger-tight), then 30 Nm (final)
   - **Why star pattern?** Prevents disc from shifting off-center during tightening

5. Remove alignment tool carefully

**Challenge 2: Alignment Tool Stuck**

Arjun tries to remove the alignment tool—it's stuck.

"Sir, the tool won't come out."

Ramesh inspects: "You torqued the pressure plate bolts with the tool still in place. The diaphragm spring clamped down on the disc, pinching the tool. Loosen two bolts, remove tool, then re-torque."

**Lesson Learned:** Remove alignment tool BEFORE final torque, or loosen pressure plate if needed.

**Step 4: Install New Release Bearing**
- Clean release bearing sleeve on transmission input shaft
- Apply thin coat of high-temp grease to sleeve
- Install new hydraulic release bearing (clips onto clutch fork or slave cylinder)
- Verify bearing slides smoothly on sleeve

**11:45 AM - Clutch Assembly Complete**

## Assessment Content

### Quiz Questions (5 Questions - PowerPoint Slide Format)

**Question 1: Clutch Diagnosis (Analyze Level)**

A customer reports: "Clutch pedal engagement very high, slipping under acceleration, burning smell occasionally."

What is the MOST likely cause?

A) Hydraulic clutch master cylinder failure
B) Worn clutch disc (friction material worn thin)
C) Broken pressure plate diaphragm spring
D) Flywheel warped from overheating

**Answer:** B) Worn clutch disc (friction material worn thin)

**Explanation:** High engagement point occurs as clutch disc wears—pressure plate travels farther to compensate, raising engagement. Slipping under load indicates friction material can't transmit torque. Burning smell is friction material overheating during slip. These three symptoms together confirm worn clutch disc. Master cylinder failure causes low/spongy pedal. Broken diaphragm spring prevents engagement entirely. Warped flywheel causes chatter/vibration, not slipping.

---

**Question 2: Clutch Installation Procedure (Apply Level)**

When installing a clutch disc and pressure plate, what is the CORRECT sequence?

A) Torque pressure plate bolts, then insert alignment tool
B) Install pressure plate, insert alignment tool, torque bolts in star pattern, remove tool
C) Install clutch disc, insert alignment tool through disc hub, install and torque pressure plate, remove tool
D) Install pressure plate loosely, remove alignment tool, then torque bolts

**Answer:** C) Install clutch disc, insert alignment tool through disc hub, install and torque pressure plate, remove tool

**Explanation:** Correct sequence: (1) Place disc on flywheel, (2) Insert alignment tool through disc hub into pilot bearing to center disc, (3) Place pressure plate over disc, (4) Torque bolts in star pattern, (5) Remove alignment tool. This ensures disc remains centered during pressure plate installation. Installing pressure plate before alignment tool is inserted won't center disc. Removing tool before torque allows disc to shift.

---

**Question 3: Contamination Diagnosis (Analyze Level)**

During clutch inspection, you find oil on the clutch disc friction material. What are the two most common sources of this oil contamination on a Maruti Swift?

A) Engine oil filler cap leak and valve cover gasket
B) Rear main seal (engine) or transmission input shaft seal
C) Clutch master cylinder leak and slave cylinder leak
D) Differential oil seal and axle seal

**Answer:** B) Rear main seal (engine) or transmission input shaft seal

**Explanation:** Oil on clutch disc comes from seals between engine and transmission. Rear main seal (engine crankshaft seal) and transmission input shaft seal are the only two sources that allow oil to migrate onto clutch disc inside bell housing. Valve cover and oil filler are external. Clutch hydraulic fluid is different from oil. Differential is not connected to clutch housing on FWD vehicles.

**Question 4: Flywheel Service Decision (Apply Level)**

You measure a Maruti Swift flywheel and find:
- Thickness: 17.2mm (minimum specification: 16.0mm)
- Surface condition: Light scoring, no cracks or hot spots
- Runout: 0.05mm (maximum specification: 0.15mm)

Resurfacing removes 0.3-0.5mm material and costs ₹800. Customer needs car same day.

What is the BEST recommendation?

A) Replace flywheel (₹4,500)
B) Resurface flywheel (₹800, 2-hour delay)
C) Install clutch on existing flywheel after cleaning friction surface
D) Install clutch without cleaning (light scoring is acceptable)

**Answer:** C) Install clutch on existing flywheel after cleaning friction surface

**Explanation:** Flywheel meets all specifications: thickness above minimum (17.2mm > 16.0mm), runout acceptable (0.05mm < 0.15mm), no severe damage. Light scoring is acceptable—cleaning with brake cleaner and Scotch-Brite removes glazing, improving friction. This saves customer ₹800 and meets same-day deadline without compromising quality. Replacement is unnecessary (expensive, flywheel functional). Resurfacing is optional but causes delay. Cleaning is essential—improves friction and clutch life.

---

**Question 5: Hydraulic Clutch Bleeding (Apply Level)**

After replacing a clutch on a hydraulically-operated clutch system (Maruti Swift), the clutch pedal feels spongy and clutch does not release fully. What is the MOST likely cause and solution?

A) Clutch disc installed backwards—remove transmission and reverse disc orientation
B) Air in hydraulic line—bleed clutch hydraulic system until pedal firm
C) Pressure plate bolts not torqued—re-torque to specification
D) Release bearing not installed—remove transmission and install bearing

**Answer:** B) Air in hydraulic line—bleed clutch hydraulic system until pedal firm

**Explanation:** Spongy pedal on hydraulic clutch indicates air in hydraulic line (air compresses, fluid doesn't). When slave cylinder was disconnected during transmission removal, air entered system. Solution: Bleed clutch hydraulic system (same procedure as brakes) until no air bubbles visible and pedal feels firm. Disc orientation doesn't affect pedal feel. Loose pressure plate bolts cause slipping, not spongy pedal. Missing release bearing would create noise and poor release, but pedal would still feel firm.

### Hands-On Exercise Preview (Afternoon Practical - 4 Hours)

**Exercise Title:** Maruti Swift Clutch Replacement (Real Vehicle Service)

**Objective:**
Remove transmission, replace clutch kit (disc, pressure plate, release bearing), inspect and service flywheel, install transmission, bleed hydraulic clutch, and verify proper operation through road test.

**Step-by-Step Activity:**

**Setup (15 min):**
1. Students divided into groups of 4
2. Each group receives:
   - Maruti Swift or similar vehicle (with worn clutch requiring replacement)
   - Complete clutch kit (disc, pressure plate, release bearing)
   - Tool set (transmission jack, torque wrench, alignment tool, clutch bleeding kit)
   - Measurement tools (micrometer, dial indicator, feeler gauges)
   - Service manual with torque specifications
   - Job card with customer scenario

**Phase 1 - Diagnosis and Road Test (30 min):**
1. Read customer complaint on job card
2. Perform pre-service road test (verify slip, engagement point, pedal feel)
3. Measure clutch pedal free play
4. Document findings on inspection sheet
5. Instructor reviews diagnosis before proceeding

**Phase 2 - Transmission Removal (60 min):**
6. Lift vehicle, secure on safety locks
7. Disconnect battery, drain transmission oil
8. Photograph shift linkage and connections before removal
9. Disconnect all systems (shift linkage, clutch hydraulic, sensors, driveshafts)
10. Support transmission with jack
11. Remove bell housing bolts (note lengths and positions)
12. Carefully slide transmission straight back and lower
13. Instructor checks: No damage to components, safe procedure followed

**Phase 3 - Clutch Inspection and Measurement (30 min):**
14. Measure clutch disc thickness using micrometer
15. Inspect friction material for wear, oil contamination, cracks
16. Inspect pressure plate for cracks, hot spots, worn friction surface
17. Measure flywheel thickness and runout using dial indicator
18. Check for oil leaks (rear main seal, input shaft seal)
19. Record all measurements on worksheet
20. Make service recommendations (resurface/replace flywheel, address oil leaks)

**Phase 4 - Clutch Installation (45 min):**
21. Clean flywheel friction surface (brake cleaner + Scotch-Brite)
22. Replace transmission input shaft seal if leaking
23. Install clutch disc on flywheel (verify correct orientation—"Flywheel Side" marking)
24. Insert alignment tool through disc hub into pilot bearing
25. Install pressure plate over disc, align bolt holes
26. Install pressure plate bolts finger-tight
27. Torque pressure plate bolts in star pattern (15 Nm, then 30 Nm final)
28. Remove alignment tool
29. Install new release bearing on input shaft sleeve (apply high-temp grease)
30. Instructor checks: Disc orientation, pressure plate torque, release bearing installation

**Phase 5 - Transmission Installation (45 min):**
31. Apply light grease to transmission input shaft splines
32. Support transmission on jack, align with engine
33. Carefully slide transmission forward (input shaft enters clutch disc hub)
   - If resistance: STOP, re-align using alignment tool from transmission side
34. Install bell housing bolts (correct lengths in correct positions)
35. Torque bell housing bolts: 55 Nm in star pattern
36. Install transmission and engine mounts
37. Install driveshafts (align marks from disassembly)
38. Connect all systems (shift linkage, clutch hydraulic, sensors)
39. Fill transmission with correct oil type and quantity (2.0L 75W-90 GL-4)
40. Install wheels, lower vehicle, reconnect battery

**Phase 6 - Hydraulic Clutch Bleeding (20 min):**
41. Attach clear tube to slave cylinder bleeder valve
42. Assistant depresses clutch pedal and holds
43. Open bleeder valve—fluid and air bubbles flow out
44. Close bleeder valve, assistant releases pedal
45. Repeat until no air bubbles visible (typically 5-6 cycles)
46. Verify pedal feels firm with proper free play (15-20mm)
47. Top off clutch master cylinder reservoir as needed

**Phase 7 - Road Test and Verification (20 min):**
48. Instructor accompanies student on road test
49. Test clutch engagement (smooth, proper pedal position)
50. Perform slip test (3rd gear, full throttle—no RPM rise without speed increase)
51. Verify shift quality (all gears engage smoothly, no grinding)
52. Listen for noise (no squeaking from release bearing, no rattling)
53. Complete final inspection checklist
54. Instructor signs off on completed work

**Success Criteria:**
- **Diagnosis Accuracy:** Correct identification of clutch wear and additional findings (oil leaks)
- **Measurement Accuracy:** All measurements within ±0.05mm (disc thickness, flywheel runout)
- **Installation Quality:** Clutch disc correctly oriented, pressure plate torqued to spec, transmission installs smoothly
- **Hydraulic Bleeding:** Pedal firm with no air, free play within 15-20mm specification
- **Road Test:** No slipping under load, smooth engagement, no noise or vibration
- **Time Management:** Complete job within 4.5 hours (simulates real workshop deadline)
- **Safety Compliance:** 100% adherence to safety procedures (lift locks, battery disconnect, proper lifting)

**Common Challenges (Instructor Will Address):**

1. **Transmission Won't Slide onto Clutch:** Clutch disc misaligned
   - **Solution:** Insert alignment tool from transmission side, rotate to center, try again

2. **Clutch Disc Installed Backwards:** Friction side facing wrong direction
   - **Solution:** Check for "Flywheel Side" marking or hub offset direction before installation

3. **Air in Hydraulic System:** Spongy pedal after installation
   - **Solution:** Bleed clutch hydraulic system thoroughly, check master cylinder fluid level

4. **Oil Contamination Missed:** New clutch slips prematurely
   - **Solution:** Always inspect for oil sources (rear main seal, input shaft seal) during disassembly

5. **Pressure Plate Bolts Unevenly Torqued:** Clutch chatter or disc damage
   - **Solution:** Always torque in star pattern, two stages (initial + final)

**Safety Reminders:**
- Transmission is heavy (40-50 kg)—use proper jack support and lifting technique
- Driveshafts must be supported—do not let hang (damages CV joints)
