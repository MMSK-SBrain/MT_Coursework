## Content Framework

### Part 1: The Challenge - Understanding Modern Transmissions (15 minutes)

**Instructor Kumar Sir Begins:**

"Good morning. Today we're covering two transmission types that confuse many technicians: **AMT** and **CVT**. Before we service them, we need to understand how they work. But here's the problem—they work completely differently from any transmission you've learned."

**The Confusion:**

**Student Question:** "Sir, I've heard customers say 'My car has automatic transmission' but it jerks when shifting. Others say 'My automatic is so smooth, no jerks at all.' Why the difference?"

"Excellent question. Because 'automatic' is a broad term. Let me show you three types:"

**The Three Types (Quick Overview):**

1. **Conventional Automatic (Torque Converter):** Maruti Ciaz AT, Honda City CVT... wait, City uses CVT, not conventional
2. **AMT (Automated Manual):** Maruti Alto AMT, WagonR AMT, Celerio AMT
3. **CVT (Continuously Variable):** Honda City, Jazz, Amaze; Nissan Magnite, Kicks

"Same word—'automatic'—three completely different machines. Let me use analogies to explain."

---

### Part 2: The Analogy - Making Complex Simple (45 minutes)

**Analogy 1: The Manual Transmission = Staircase (Baseline)**

"You all understand manual transmission. Let me use a visual analogy."

**The Staircase Analogy:**

"Imagine driving is like climbing stairs:
- **1st gear** = 1st step (low, easy to start)
- **2nd gear** = 2nd step
- **3rd gear** = 3rd step
...and so on to 5th gear.

To go from 1st to 3rd, you MUST step on 2nd. You can't skip steps. Each shift is distinct. You feel it: Clutch in, change gear, clutch out. That's manual transmission—**discrete steps, like a staircase.**"

---

**Analogy 2: AMT = Robot Operating the Manual Staircase**

**Student Question:** "Sir, what is AMT exactly?"

"AMT is simple: **Take a manual transmission (same staircase), but let a robot press the clutch and move the gear lever instead of you.**"

**The Robot Analogy:**

"Imagine you're climbing the staircase (manual transmission). Normally, YOU decide when to step up, and YOU move your legs. AMT is like having a robot assistant:
- Robot watches your speed (ECU monitors vehicle speed)
- Robot decides 'time to shift' (shift logic in ECU)
- Robot presses clutch (hydraulic or electric actuator)
- Robot moves gear lever (shift actuator)
- Robot releases clutch (clutch actuator)

**Same gearbox, same clutch, same steps—just automated control.**"

**Why It Jerks:**

"The robot isn't as smooth as you. When you shift, you can feel the engine load and release clutch gradually. The robot uses pre-programmed timing. Sometimes it's abrupt. That's why AMT has a reputation for jerky shifts—especially in traffic."

**Real Example (Maruti Alto AMT):**

"Let me show you the Alto AMT cutaway. See this? Manual 5-speed gearbox (same as manual Alto). But mounted on top: Actuators. One for clutch, one for gear selection. ECU controls both."

**Components:**
- Hydraulic clutch actuator (presses/releases clutch)
- Gear shift actuator (moves gear lever electrically)
- ECU (Transmission Control Unit)
- Gear position sensors
- Clutch position sensor

"That's AMT. **Manual transmission + robot control = Automated Manual Transmission.**"

---

**Analogy 3: CVT = Bicycle Gears That Adjust Infinitely**

**Student Question:** "What about CVT? How is it different?"

"CVT is revolutionary. Forget the staircase. CVT is more like a **bicycle with infinitely adjustable gears.**"

**The Bicycle Analogy:**

"You know how bicycles have multiple gears—you shift from 1 to 10. But imagine a bicycle where the gears adjust **smoothly** from 1 to 10 with infinite steps in between: 1.0, 1.5, 2.3, 3.7, 4.9... **no jumps, no steps, just continuous adjustment.**

That's CVT. No fixed gears. **Infinite gear ratios between minimum and maximum.**"

**How It Works (Simple Explanation):**

"CVT uses two pulleys and a metal belt (or chain):
1. **Drive pulley** (connected to engine)
2. **Driven pulley** (connected to wheels)
3. **Metal belt** (connects both pulleys)

**The Magic:**
- Pulleys can change diameter (sides squeeze together = smaller diameter; sides spread apart = larger diameter)
- When drive pulley is small and driven pulley is large → Low gear (like 1st gear)
- When drive pulley is large and driven pulley is small → High gear (like 5th gear)
- **Pulleys adjust infinitely smoothly → infinite gear ratios**

No clutch. No gear shifting. Just continuous ratio change."

**Visual Demo:**

Kumar sir shows a CVT cutaway model. "See these cone-shaped pulleys? The belt rides at different positions. When pulleys adjust, the belt moves up or down the cone, changing effective diameter. **Ratio changes continuously.**"

---

**Analogy 4: Comparing the Three**

**The Summary Analogy:**

"Let me use one final analogy to compare all three:

**Manual Transmission = Climbing stairs**
- Distinct steps (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th)
- You control when to step up
- You feel every shift

**AMT = Robot climbing stairs for you**
- Same stairs (same gearbox)
- Robot decides when to shift
- Robot moves your legs (actuators)
- Still feels like steps (jerky shifts)

**CVT = Riding an escalator**
- No steps, smooth continuous movement
- Automatically adjusts speed
- No jerks, no shifts—seamless

**Conventional Automatic = Elevator with preset floors**
- Smooth (torque converter cushions shifts)
- Predetermined floors (fixed gear ratios: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th)
- More comfortable than stairs, smoother than robot"

### Part 3: The Application - Service Procedures (60 minutes)

**AMT Service (30 minutes)**

**Key Point:** "AMT uses standard manual transmission internals. Service clutch and gearbox like manual, PLUS service actuators and perform adaptation."

**Service Procedure 1: Clutch Adaptation**

**Why Needed:**
"Clutch wears over time. Engagement point changes. ECU must relearn clutch bite point after clutch replacement or when shifts become jerky."

**Procedure (Maruti AMT Example):**
1. Connect scan tool
2. Navigate to AMT system
3. Select "Clutch Adaptation"
4. Follow prompts:
   - Engine at operating temp
   - Transmission in neutral
   - Vehicle stationary
   - Brake applied
5. ECU cycles clutch actuator through full range
6. ECU learns minimum/maximum positions and bite point
7. Adaptation complete (takes 2-3 minutes)

**Service Procedure 2: Actuator Inspection**

**Hydraulic Clutch Actuator:**
- Check fluid level (uses brake fluid)
- Inspect for leaks at actuator seals
- Check actuator response (using scan tool active test)

**Gear Shift Actuator:**
- Listen for unusual noise during shifts
- Check actuator motor current (high current = worn motor)
- Inspect cables/linkages for wear

**Common AMT Issues:**
- Jerky shifts → Clutch adaptation required
- Won't shift into gear → Actuator failure
- Clutch smell → Actuator holding clutch partially engaged

---

**CVT Service (30 minutes)**

**Key Point:** "CVT fluid is critical. Wrong fluid = belt slip = transmission failure."

**Service Procedure 1: CVT Fluid Change**

**Why Specific Fluid?**

"CVT fluid has friction modifiers that allow metal belt to grip pulleys without slipping. Using ATF or engine oil causes belt slip and rapid wear. **Always use Honda CVTF for Honda CVT, manufacturer-specific fluid.**"

**Procedure (Honda City CVT Example):**

1. **Drain old fluid:**
   - Remove drain plug (17mm bolt)
   - Drain approximately 3.5 liters
   - **Important:** CVT doesn't drain completely (7 liters total capacity, only 3.5 drains)

2. **Refill with Honda CVTF:**
   - Add 3.5 liters through dipstick tube (some CVTs have fill plug instead)

3. **Fluid level check:**
   - Engine running, transmission in P
   - Fluid at operating temp (70-80°C)
   - Check level on dipstick (HOT range)
   - **Critical:** Overfilling causes foaming and belt slip

4. **Test drive:**
   - Drive 10 km to circulate new fluid
   - Check for smooth operation, no slipping

**Service Interval:** 40,000 km (Honda recommendation; severe duty: 30,000 km)

---

**Service Procedure 2: Pulley Inspection (During Overhaul)**

**When to Inspect:**
- CVT slipping (RPM increases but speed doesn't)
- Whining noise
- Shuddering during acceleration

**What to Check:**
1. **Pulley surfaces:** Should be mirror-smooth, no scoring
2. **Metal belt:** Check for cracks, stretched links
3. **Belt wear:** Measure belt width (worn belt = narrower)
4. **Stepper motor:** Controls pulley position, check for binding

**Common CVT Failures:**
- Belt slip (low/contaminated fluid)
- Pulley wear (abrasive particles in fluid)
- Stepper motor failure (pulleys stuck, no ratio change)

- Budget-conscious buyers wanting convenience

- Driving engagement, control

## Technical Deep Dive

### AMT Components

**Actuators:**
1. **Clutch Actuator:** Hydraulic cylinder or electric motor (replaces your left foot)
2. **Gear Shift Actuator:** Electric motor with position sensors (replaces your hand)
3. **TCU (Transmission Control Unit):** Controls actuator timing based on speed, throttle, load

**Clutch Adaptation Parameters:**
- Bite point position (mm)
- Full engagement position (mm)
- Release position (mm)
- Clutch wear compensation

---

### CVT Components

**Primary Components:**
1. **Drive Pulley (Primary):** Connected to engine, variable diameter
2. **Driven Pulley (Secondary):** Connected to final drive, variable diameter
3. **Metal Belt (or Chain):** Links pulleys, transmits power
4. **Hydraulic Pressure System:** Squeezes pulleys to change diameter
5. **Stepper Motor/Solenoids:** Control hydraulic pressure to each pulley

**Operating Principle:**
- Low gear: Drive pulley small, driven pulley large (belt at small diameter on drive, large on driven)
- High gear: Drive pulley large, driven pulley small (belt at large diameter on drive, small on driven)
- Ratio adjustment: Pulleys adjust simultaneously (one gets smaller as other gets larger)

## Assessment Content

### Quiz Questions (5 Questions)

**Question 1: AMT Understanding (Understand Level)**

What is the PRIMARY difference between AMT and conventional manual transmission?

A) AMT has torque converter, manual has clutch
B) AMT has automated actuators for clutch and gear shifting
C) AMT has infinite gear ratios
D) AMT uses CVT technology

**Answer:** B) AMT has automated actuators for clutch and gear shifting
**Explanation:** AMT = Automated Manual Transmission. Same gearbox as manual, same clutch, but actuators automate clutch operation and gear shifting. No torque converter (that's conventional automatic). No infinite ratios (that's CVT).

---

**Question 2: CVT Fluid Importance (Understand Level)**

Why must you use manufacturer-specific CVT fluid and never substitute with ATF?

A) CVT fluid is cheaper
B) CVT fluid has friction modifiers that prevent belt slip
C) ATF damages CVT seals
D) No technical reason, manufacturer preference only

**Answer:** B) CVT fluid has friction modifiers that prevent belt slip
**Explanation:** CVT uses metal belt on pulleys. Requires precise friction characteristics. CVT fluid engineered to allow controlled grip without slip. ATF (for torque converter automatics) has different friction modifiers—causes belt slip, rapid wear, transmission failure.

---

**Question 3: AMT Clutch Adaptation (Apply Level)**

Customer complains AMT shifts are becoming jerky after clutch replacement. What service procedure is required?

A) Replace transmission control unit
B) Perform clutch adaptation using scan tool
C) Replace clutch actuator
D) Adjust clutch cable (no adjustment needed)

**Answer:** B) Perform clutch adaptation using scan tool
**Explanation:** After clutch replacement, engagement point changes. ECU must relearn bite point. Clutch adaptation procedure (via scan tool) cycles actuator through range, ECU learns new positions. Resolves jerky shifts. Takes 2-3 minutes.

---

**Question 4: CVT Fluid Level Check (Apply Level)**

When checking CVT fluid level on Honda City, what are the correct conditions?

A) Engine off, cold, transmission in Park
B) Engine running, fluid at operating temp (70-80°C), transmission in Park
C) Engine running, cold, transmission in Neutral
D) Engine off, hot, transmission in Drive

**Answer:** B) Engine running, fluid at operating temp (70-80°C), transmission in Park
**Explanation:** CVT fluid level must be checked hot (operating temp) with engine running (pump circulating fluid) and transmission in Park. Cold check or engine-off check gives inaccurate level. Overfilling causes foaming and slip.

---

**Question 5: Transmission Type Identification (Analyze Level)**

Customer says "My automatic transmission has no gears, just smooth acceleration. What type is it?"

A) Conventional automatic with torque converter
B) AMT (Automated Manual)
C) CVT (Continuously Variable)
D) Manual with automatic clutch

**Answer:** C) CVT (Continuously Variable)
**Explanation:** "No gears" and "smooth acceleration" = CVT. CVT has infinite ratios, no fixed gears, seamless acceleration. AMT has distinct gears (feels like manual, jerky). Conventional automatic has gears (smooth but you feel 4-6 shifts).
